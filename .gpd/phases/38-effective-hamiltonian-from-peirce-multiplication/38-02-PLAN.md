  <files>code/effective_hamiltonian.py</files>
  <action>
    Determine whether H_eff = J sum T_a^(i) T_a^(j) has full F_4 symmetry or only Spin(9) symmetry.

    Strategy: Use two complementary approaches.

    **Approach A (algebraic/structural):** The 9 traceless T_a transform as the VECTOR representation of Spin(9) (9-dim). The Heisenberg coupling sum T_a^(1) T_a^(2) is the Spin(9)-invariant contraction of two copies of the vector representation. Under the FULL F_4, the 16-dim representation V_{1/2} is NOT the spinor of Spin(9) alone -- F_4 has 52 generators, 36 of which are Spin(9). The remaining 16 generators transform V_{1/2} in ways that mix the octonionic directions. The Heisenberg coupling uses only the 9 Cl(9) vector generators (which transform as the 9-dim vector rep of Spin(9)). Under F_4, these 9 generators are part of the 26-dim representation (the traceless part of h_3(O)). The coupling sum T_a^(1) T_a^(2) sums over only 9 of the 26 components of this representation. If the 16 generators outside Spin(9) permute the T_a among themselves AND among the other 17 components, then H_eff (which sums only 9 of them) would NOT be F_4-invariant.

    The key algebraic argument: F_4 acts on the 26-dim traceless part of h_3(O). The V_0 = h_2(O) subspace is 10-dim. Under Spin(9) subset F_4, V_0 decomposes as 1 (trace) + 9 (vector). Under full F_4, V_0 is NOT an invariant subspace -- F_4 mixes V_0 with V_{1/2}. Therefore, the projection onto V_0 (which defines the T_b operators) is Spin(9)-invariant but NOT F_4-invariant. Consequently, H_eff = sum T_a^(1) T_a^(2) is Spin(9)-invariant but NOT F_4-invariant. The frame stabilizer IS Spin(9), not full F_4.

    Verify this argument computationally by constructing the 16 extra F_4 generators outside Spin(9).

    **Approach B (computational):** The 16 generators of F_4 outside Spin(9) act on V_{1/2} = R^{16}. By the Baez 2002 structure (Sec. 3.4), these correspond to the V_{1/2} directions in the Lie algebra f_4. On V_{1/2}, these generators have a specific form: they are the maps v -> Pi_{1/2}(a o v) for a in V_{1/2} (Peirce multiplication by off-diagonal elements). However, this gives maps V_{1/2} -> V_0 + V_1 (not V_{1/2} -> V_{1/2}). The correct F_4 generators on V_{1/2} come from the f_4 representation on the 16-dim space.

    Actually, the most efficient route: use the existing operator algebra. From Phase 28, the commutator algebra of {T_a} gives 36-dim spin(9). The FULL f_4 acting on V_{1/2} is 52-dim. But f_4 acts on the 27-dim h_3(O), not on V_{1/2} alone. The restriction of f_4 to V_{1/2} gives a 52-dim Lie algebra acting on R^{16}, but the action factors through spin(9) + the 16 "extra" generators. These 16 extra generators, when restricted to V_{1/2}, may act trivially or nontrivially.

    Most efficient verification: use the 2-site spectrum from Plan 01. Check whether the eigenvalue degeneracies are consistent with Spin(9) irreps or require F_4 irreps for explanation.

    - If degeneracies match Spin(9) irreps exactly: stabilizer is Spin(9)
    - If some degeneracies combine into larger F_4 irreps: stabilizer might be larger
    - If a subset matches Spin(8) or G_2 irreps: stabilizer could be smaller

    Implement function frame_stabilizer_analysis(eigenvalues, eigenvectors, T_matrices) that:
    (a) Uses the algebraic argument (projection onto V_0 breaks F_4)
    (b) Cross-checks with spectrum degeneracy analysis
    (c) Reports the stabilizer with confidence level

    Also implement test_f4_beyond_spin9(H_2, T_matrices) that attempts to construct the extra 16 generators by using the existing code (krasnov_J_u_matrix and related functions give ONE antisymmetric 16x16 matrix that is NOT in spin(9)). The extra F_4 generators on V_{1/2} are related to the Peirce multiplication maps. Check if J_u (which we know from Phase 28) commutes with H_2 -- if [H_2, J_u tensor I + I tensor J_u] != 0, that directly proves H_eff does NOT have full F_4 symmetry.
  </action>

    Write the Phase 38 derivation document covering three topics: lattice structure resolution, cubic det(A) assessment, and Phase 39 handoff.

    **Section 1: Lattice Structure Resolution**

    State the argument explicitly (each numbered step must be justified):

    1. Each self-modeler's internal algebra is h_3(O). The Peirce decomposition under E_11 gives h_3(O) = V_1 + V_{1/2} + V_0 with dims 1 + 16 + 10 = 27. This is the ON-SITE algebraic structure.

    2. The K_3 Peirce graph (three idempotents E_11, E_22, E_33 connected by off-diagonal spaces V_12, V_13, V_23) describes how components of a SINGLE h_3(O) are related. It is NOT a lattice of self-modelers.

    3. Many self-modelers form a network. The physical lattice has one node per self-modeler. For a regular lattice in d dimensions, this is Z^d. Each node carries an h_3(O) (equivalently, its V_{1/2} = R^{16} degree of freedom for the bilinear coupling).

    4. Nearest-neighbor interaction between sites i and j acts on V_{1/2}^(i) tensor V_{1/2}^(j) via H_{ij} = J sum T_a^(i) T_a^(j).

    5. The physical lattice Z^d IS bipartite: partition into A-sublattice (sites where sum of integer coordinates is even) and B-sublattice (sum is odd). Every nearest-neighbor bond connects A to B. This is the standard checkerboard decomposition.

    6. DLS reflection positivity applies to the physical lattice Z^d, NOT to the internal Peirce graph K_3. The K_3 non-bipartiteness is irrelevant because K_3 describes the internal algebraic structure at each site, not the spatial arrangement of sites.

    **Section 2: Cubic det(A) Assessment**

    1. The cubic determinant det(A) = abc + 2Re(x_1 x_2 x_3) - a|x_1|^2 - b|x_2|^2 - c|x_3|^2 is the unique cubic F_4-invariant of h_3(O).

    2. In the lattice model, det(A) enters as an ON-SITE potential (single-site term) or a 3-BODY interaction (involving 3 sites), NOT as a nearest-neighbor bilinear. The Jordan product a o b = (1/2)(ab + ba) is bilinear; the determinant is cubic.

    3. For the bilinear Hamiltonian H_eff = J sum T_a^(i) T_a^(j), the cubic term is absent by construction.

    4. RG relevance estimate: In the sigma model on F_4/Spin(9) = OP^2 (16-dim target), the field phi has engineering dimension [phi] = (d-2)/2 in d spatial dimensions. A cubic term has dimension 3*(d-2)/2. The cubic is RELEVANT if 3*(d-2)/2 < d, i.e., d < 6. So in d=3, the cubic term has scaling dimension 3/2 < 3, meaning it IS relevant in the RG sense.

    5. However, relevance does not mean it is present. The cubic term must be generated by the lattice coupling. Since the nearest-neighbor coupling is bilinear, the cubic enters only through:
       (a) On-site anharmonicity (always present from h_3(O) structure)
       (b) Multi-site interactions generated under RG coarse-graining
    6. Assessment: The cubic is formally relevant in d=3 but its coefficient depends on the microscopic coupling. If the coefficient vanishes by symmetry or is parametrically small, the bilinear sigma model controls the long-distance physics. This is analogous to how phi^3 terms in O(N) models are forbidden by the Z_2 symmetry phi -> -phi. For F_4/Spin(9), determine whether there is an analogous symmetry that forbids the cubic.

    Key point: The lattice Z^d has a site-inversion symmetry (A_i -> -A_i on the Neel sublattice for antiferromagnets). Under this transformation, the cubic det(A) -> -det(A) (odd power). So the cubic term IS forbidden in the effective action by the sublattice symmetry of the antiferromagnetic ground state. This is the decisive argument.

    - Cubic: RG-relevant but symmetry-forbidden in AFM ground state
    - Goldstone mode count prediction: dim(F_4) - dim(Spin(9)) = 52 - 36 = 16
