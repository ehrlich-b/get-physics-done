      O = C + C^3 (as a REAL vector space, O = R^2 + R^6 = R^8)

    **Step 3: The volume form omega_6.**

    Define the volume element of Cl(6):
      omega_6 = gamma_1 gamma_2 gamma_3 gamma_4 gamma_5 gamma_6

    Properties to derive:
    (a) omega_6^2: Using gamma_i gamma_j = -gamma_j gamma_i for i != j and gamma_i^2 = 1:
        omega_6^2 = (gamma_1...gamma_6)(gamma_1...gamma_6)
        Moving gamma_1 past gamma_2, ..., gamma_6 costs 5 sign flips: gamma_1^2 * (-1)^5 * (gamma_2...gamma_6)^2
        Continuing: total sign = (-1)^{6*5/2} = (-1)^{15} = -1
        So omega_6^2 = -1.

    **Step 5: Pati-Salam subgroup from omega_6.**

    The volume form omega_6 breaks Spin(10) to its stabilizer. The 10 Cl(10) generators split as:
      - 6 generators gamma_1, ..., gamma_6 from Cl(6) (the Im(C^3) directions)
      - 4 remaining generators from the complement (u = e_7 and 3 additional directions)

    The Lie algebra spin(10) is spanned by {(1/4)[Gamma_A, Gamma_B] : A < B}. The stabilizer of omega_6 consists of those spin(10) elements that commute with omega_6.

    gamma_i (i=1,...,6) anticommutes with omega_6 for each i (since omega_6 = gamma_1...gamma_6 and gamma_i anticommutes with itself appearing once in the product).

    Therefore:
    - Generators [gamma_i, gamma_j] for i,j in {1,...,6} COMMUTE with omega_6 (even number of anticommutations). These generate spin(6) = su(4). Dimension: C(6,2) = 15.
    - Generators [Gamma_A, Gamma_B] for A,B in {7,8,9,10} COMMUTE with omega_6 (don't involve any Cl(6) generator). These generate spin(4) = su(2) + su(2). Dimension: C(4,2) = 6.
    - Mixed generators [gamma_i, Gamma_A] for i in {1,...,6}, A in {7,...,10} ANTICOMMUTE with omega_6. These are NOT in the stabilizer.

    Stabilizer = spin(6) + spin(4) = su(4) + su(2)_L + su(2)_R, dim = 15 + 6 = 21.

    At the group level: Stab_{Spin(10)}(omega_6) = Spin(6) x Spin(4) / Z_2 = SU(4) x SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R / Z_2.

    This is exactly the Pati-Salam group.

    **Step 6: Decomposition of the 16 under Pati-Salam.**

    The 16-dim S_{10}^+ (one Weyl spinor of Spin(10)) decomposes under SU(4) x SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R as:
      16 -> (4, 2, 1) + (4bar, 1, 2)

    Interpretation:
    - (4, 2, 1): 4 colors (3 color + 1 lepton) x 2 weak isospin (left-handed doublet) x SU(2)_R singlet
    - (4bar, 1, 2): 4bar colors x SU(2)_L singlet x 2 weak isospin (right-handed doublet)

    This is CHIRAL: SU(2)_L acts only on the (4,2,1) component. The left-handed fermions form SU(2)_L doublets while right-handed fermions are SU(2)_L singlets. This is the Standard Model chirality pattern.

    **Step 7: Breaking SU(4) -> SU(3) x U(1).**

    The SAME complex structure u that gave us Cl(6) further breaks SU(4):
    - SU(4) = Spin(6) acts on C^3 + C (the 4-dim fundamental representation)
    - The complex structure u distinguishes C (the lepton direction) from C^3 (the quark directions)
    - Stabilizer of this distinction: SU(3) x U(1) (color group x hypercharge)

    After this breaking, the SM gauge group is:
      SU(3)_color x SU(2)_L x U(1)_Y

    The U(1)_Y (hypercharge) is a specific combination of the U(1) from SU(4) -> SU(3) x U(1) and the Cartan of SU(2)_R.

    KEY POINT: This is the LEFT embedding, NOT the diagonal embedding. In the diagonal embedding, SU(2) would act on BOTH chiralities (not SM). In our construction, SU(2)_L acts ONLY on left-handed fermions because the volume form omega_6 already distinguished the chiralities before the Pati-Salam breaking.

    Cite Baez n-Category Cafe Part 13 for the Sawin theorem distinguishing left from diagonal embeddings. Cite Todorov 2022 for the Pati-Salam framework.
