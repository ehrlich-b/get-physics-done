  <name>Task 1: Derive the Luders CPTP channel and entropy change for 2-qubit SWAP</name>
  <files>derivations/23-luders-channel-entropy.md</files>
  <action>
  Derive the CPTP channel induced by the Luders sequential product on M_2(C) x M_2(C) with SWAP Hamiltonian h = JF.

  There are two distinct physical interpretations of "one step" that must both be analyzed:

  **Interpretation A -- Algebraic sequential product step:**
  The Luders sequential product a & rho = sqrt(a) rho sqrt(a) where a is an effect (0 <= a <= I) on the composite system. For the self-modeling context, a is a measurement effect of the body B on the composite B x M. The induced channel on B's reduced state is:

  E_a(rho_B) = Tr_M[sqrt(a)(rho_B tensor rho_M)sqrt(a)] / Tr[sqrt(a)(rho_B tensor rho_M)sqrt(a)]

  This is a SELECTIVE channel (post-selection on outcome a). It is NOT trace-preserving in general. Compute it explicitly for a = projector onto SWAP-eigenstates.

  **Interpretation B -- Unitary SWAP evolution step:**
  The time evolution U(t) = exp(-iJFt) for one step duration tau, followed by partial trace. The channel on B is:

  E_U(rho_B) = Tr_M[U(rho_B tensor rho_M)U^dag]

  This IS trace-preserving and completely positive. Compute it explicitly.

  For BOTH interpretations:

  1. Write the SWAP operator F on C^2 tensor C^2 in the computational basis: F|ij> = |ji>. In terms of Pauli matrices: F = (1/2)(I + sigma_x tensor sigma_x + sigma_y tensor sigma_y + sigma_z tensor sigma_z). Verify eigenvalues: +1 (triplet, dim 3), -1 (singlet, dim 1).

  2. For Interpretation B (primary, as this is the lattice dynamics):
     - Compute U(t) = exp(-iJFt) = cos(Jt)I + i*sin(Jt)F (since F^2 = I... WAIT: F^2 = I on the full space? Check: F|ij> = |ji>, F^2|ij> = |ij>, yes F^2 = I).
     - Actually F has eigenvalues +1 (triplet) and -1 (singlet), so exp(-iJFt) = exp(-iJt)|triplet><triplet| + exp(+iJt)|singlet><singlet|.
     - Equivalently: U(t) = e^{-iJt}P_+ + e^{iJt}P_- where P_+ = (I+F)/2, P_- = (I-F)/2.
     - Given initial state rho_BM = rho_B tensor rho_M:
       rho_BM(t) = U(t)(rho_B tensor rho_M)U(t)^dag
     - Partial trace over M: rho_B(t) = Tr_M[rho_BM(t)]
     - Compute S(rho_B(t)) - S(rho_B(0)) for general rho_B and rho_M = I/2 (maximally mixed model).

  3. For Interpretation A:
     - Consider the measurement effect a corresponding to the Luders product in the self-modeling context.
     - The Luders channel (non-selective) sums over a complete set of measurement outcomes: E(rho) = sum_k sqrt(a_k) rho sqrt(a_k) where sum a_k = I.
     - For the self-modeling context: the measurement effects a_k are the spectral projections of the observable being measured by the body on the model.
     - Identify the relevant measurement effects from the self-modeling structure.
     - Key question: is the non-selective Luders channel E(rho) = sum_k P_k rho P_k (for projective measurements, where a_k = P_k are projections) unital? YES: sum_k P_k I P_k = sum_k P_k = I. So the non-selective projective Luders channel IS unital!
     - For general (non-projective) effects: E(rho) = sum_k sqrt(a_k) rho sqrt(a_k). Is this unital? E(I) = sum_k a_k = I. YES, the non-selective Luders channel is always unital when the effects form a resolution of the identity.

  4. KEY INSIGHT to address explicitly: The individual Luders product a & rho = sqrt(a) rho sqrt(a) is NOT trace-preserving (selective). But the non-selective channel sum_k sqrt(a_k) rho sqrt(a_k) IS both TP and unital when sum a_k = I. This distinction is critical.

  5. For the SWAP lattice dynamics: identify which of these interpretations applies.
     - The self-modeling update cycle involves: (1) body B prepares effect a, (2) sequential product a & rho_M updates the model, (3) iterate.
     - In the lattice Hamiltonian picture (Paper 6): the dynamics is unitary SWAP evolution, not selective measurement.
     - Therefore: Interpretation B (unitary evolution + partial trace) is the primary channel for the lattice dynamics.
     - But also: the iterated sequential product picture (Paper 5) involves selective updates. Both must be analyzed.

  6. Compute the reduced-state entropy change explicitly:
     - For Interpretation B with rho_M = I/2:
       rho_B(t) = Tr_M[U(t)(rho_B tensor I/2)U(t)^dag]
       Work this out in components. The result should be a linear map on rho_B (a channel).
     - Check if this channel is unital: does E(I/2) = I/2?
     - Compute S(rho_B(t)) for the parametric state rho_B(theta) = diag(cos^2(theta/2), sin^2(theta/2)).

  7. Dimensional analysis: S(rho) is dimensionless. Delta S is dimensionless. J has dimensions of energy. t has dimensions of 1/energy. Jt is dimensionless.

  8. Units: use nats (ln) per project convention. S(I/2) = ln(2) = 0.693 nats for a qubit.

  Do NOT assume the channel is unital -- prove or disprove it explicitly for each interpretation.
  Do NOT invoke DPI before checking unitality.
  CITE Paper 5 (Luders product definition) and Paper 6 (SWAP Hamiltonian) explicitly.
  </action>
  <verify>
  1. SWAP operator: F^2 = I, eigenvalues {+1, +1, +1, -1}, Tr(F) = 2 (for 4x4)
  2. Unitarity: U(t)^dag U(t) = I
  3. CPTP: channel is completely positive (Kraus form) and trace-preserving
  4. Unitality test: explicit computation of E(I/d) for both interpretations
  5. Entropy of maximally mixed qubit: S(I/2) = ln(2) = 0.693 nats
  6. Product state entropy: S(|0><0|) = 0
  7. Dimensional analysis: S, Delta S dimensionless; Jt dimensionless
  8. Limiting cases:
     - t = 0: rho_B(0) = rho_B (no change, Delta S = 0)
     - t = pi/(2J): full SWAP, rho_B -> rho_M (entropy changes to S(rho_M))
     - rho_B = I/2 initially: should remain I/2 if channel is unital
  </verify>
