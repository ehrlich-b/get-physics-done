  <action>
    Formalize how observer states interact with V_{1/2} through the Peirce structure.

    Key definitions and constructions:

    1. OBSERVER STATES: A state on M_n(C)^sa is a positive normalized linear functional omega: M_n(C)^sa -> R with omega(1) = 1. Equivalently, omega(a) = Tr(rho * a) for a density matrix rho. The C-linear extension omega^C: M_n(C) -> C extends to the full (non-self-adjoint) algebra: omega^C(a + ib) = omega(a) + i*omega(b).

    2. PEIRCE INTERFACE: The observer, via its idempotent E_11, generates Peirce projections. The Peirce multiplication maps L_{E_11}: V_{1/2} -> V_{1/2} (multiplication by 1/2). More generally, for any a in V_1, the map L_a: V_{1/2} -> V_{1/2} (defined by v -> a * v) is an endomorphism of V_{1/2}.

    3. STATE-EFFECT PAIRING: When the observer evaluates a state omega on an element v in V_{1/2}, the pairing goes through the Peirce interface. Concretely:
       - The observer "measures" v in V_{1/2} by computing omega(P_1(v * v)) or a similar Peirce-projected quantity
       - The observer can also form bilinear pairings: (v, w) -> omega(P_1(v * w)) for v, w in V_{1/2}
       - Here v * w is the Jordan product of elements in V_{1/2}, and P_1 projects to V_1

    4. PEIRCE MULTIPLICATION RULES (from Alfsen-Shultz):
       - V_1 * V_1 subset V_1
       - V_1 * V_{1/2} subset V_{1/2}
       - V_1 * V_0 = {0}
       - V_{1/2} * V_{1/2} subset V_1 + V_0
       - V_{1/2} * V_0 subset V_{1/2}
       - V_0 * V_0 subset V_0

    5. EFFECT ALGEBRA: The effects (observables in [0,1]) of the observer form E(M_n(C)^sa) = {a in M_n(C)^sa : 0 <= a <= 1}. These are C*-algebra effects. When the observer probes V_{1/2}, it constructs effects from V_{1/2} elements via the Peirce rules.

    6. STATE SEPARATION ON V_{1/2}: Analyze whether observer states can separate points in V_{1/2}. Since V_{1/2} * V_{1/2} subset V_1 + V_0, and the observer "sees" V_1 directly, the pairing v -> omega(P_1(v * w)) gives the observer access to certain R-bilinear forms on V_{1/2}.

    Question: does the C-linearity of omega^C canonically extend these R-bilinear forms to C-bilinear forms on V_{1/2}^C?

    ASSERT_CONVENTION at top: jordan_product=(1/2)(ab+ba), peirce_decomposition=under_E11, state_normalization=Tr(rho)=1

  <name>Task 2: Determine whether state-effect duality forces complexification</name>
  <files>derivations/13-route2-state-effect-duality.md</files>
  <action>
    Resolve the decisive question for Route 2.

    PATH A (theorem): State-effect duality forces complexification.
    Argument approach: The observer's C-linear extension omega^C gives a C-valued pairing on V_{1/2}. If this pairing separates points in V_{1/2} and respects a canonical complex structure, then V_{1/2} inherits a C-module structure.

    Specifically: define the C-bilinear form B^C(v, w) = omega^C(P_1^C(v * w)) where P_1^C is the complexified Peirce projection. If this form is non-degenerate on V_{1/2} tensor_R C and determines a unique complex structure J on V_{1/2} such that B^C(Jv, w) = i * B^C(v, w), then complexification is forced.

    However, there is a subtlety: the Peirce product v * w for v, w in V_{1/2} maps to V_1 + V_0. The observer only "sees" V_1. So the observer's pairing captures P_1(v * w) but not P_0(v * w). Analyze whether P_1(v * w) alone, with the observer's C-linear states applied, is sufficient to force complexification of V_{1/2}.

    For h_3(O) specifically: compute P_1(v * w) explicitly for v = (0, x_3, x_2) and w = (0, y_3, y_2) where x_i, y_i in O. The Jordan product v * w has (1,1)-entry = (1/2)(x_3 bar(y_3) + y_3 bar(x_3) + x_2 bar(y_2) + y_2 bar(x_2)). This is Re(x_3 bar(y_3)) + Re(x_2 bar(y_2)). This is a REAL inner product on V_{1/2}.

    Critical question: the observer evaluates omega(P_1(v * w)) = alpha * [Re(x_3 bar(y_3)) + Re(x_2 bar(y_2))] where alpha comes from the state. This is an R-bilinear form. Can the observer's C-linear extension upgrade this to a C-bilinear (or sesquilinear) form?

    Note: the real inner product Re(x bar(y)) on O is the standard inner product on R^8. A C-bilinear extension would require an almost complex structure J on O. But O itself does not carry a CANONICAL complex structure (it is a normed division algebra over R, not over C). This may be the key obstruction or the key insight.

    PATH B (counterexample): State-effect duality does NOT force complexification.
    If the pairing P_1(v * w) gives only a real inner product on V_{1/2} = O^2, and the C-linear extension of omega merely gives omega^C(P_1(v * w)) = alpha * [Re(x_3 bar(y_3)) + Re(x_2 bar(y_2))] (still real-valued because P_1(v * w) in V_1 = R), then the C-linear extension does NOT provide new information about V_{1/2}. The obstacle: V_1 = R, so P_1(anything) is real, and omega^C on a real element returns a real number.

    Resolution path: determine whether the observer can extract COMPLEX information from V_{1/2} through any Peirce-mediated pairing, or whether all Peirce-mediated pairings on V_{1/2} factor through V_1 = R and are therefore inherently real.

    Write the result as formal theorem or counterexample. State all hypotheses.
  </action>
