<objective>
Route 4: Determine whether the tensor product A tensor_R V_{1/2} (A = observer's C*-algebra) naturally complexifies to A tensor_C V_{1/2}^C as the correct model of "observer probes V_{1/2}."

Purpose: The most algebraically direct route to complexification. If the observer (C*-algebra A = M_n(C)) tensors with V_{1/2} over R, the resulting space A tensor_R V_{1/2} is a module over A. Since A has a C-algebra structure, this tensor product may naturally upgrade to A tensor_C V_{1/2}^C, which would force V_{1/2}^C to exist as the canonical complexification. Hanche-Olsen's theory of JC-algebra tensor products provides the framework.

Output: A derivation file containing either a theorem or counterexample.
</objective>

  <files>derivations/15-route4-tensor-product.md</files>

    Formalize the tensor product model of "C*-observer probes real Jordan module."

    1. SETUP: The observer's full algebra is A = M_n(C). Its self-adjoint part A^sa = M_n(C)^sa is the state space from Paper 5. The target module is V_{1/2} = O^2 (Peirce half-space of h_3(O) under E_11), a real vector space of dimension 16.

    2. THE R-TENSOR PRODUCT: Form A tensor_R V_{1/2}. This is a real vector space of dimension dim_R(A) * dim_R(V_{1/2}) = 2n^2 * 16. But A = M_n(C) is a C-algebra, so A tensor_R V_{1/2} is naturally a LEFT A-module: a * (sum_i a_i tensor v_i) = sum_i (a * a_i) tensor v_i.

    As a left A-module, A tensor_R V_{1/2} has a natural complex structure: for any z in C, z * (a tensor v) = (za) tensor v. This makes A tensor_R V_{1/2} a complex vector space of dimension dim_C = n^2 * 16.

    3. THE C-TENSOR PRODUCT: Now consider A tensor_C V_{1/2}^C where V_{1/2}^C = V_{1/2} tensor_R C. This is a complex vector space of dimension dim_C = n^2 * 16.

    KEY CLAIM TO INVESTIGATE: There is a canonical isomorphism A tensor_R V_{1/2} = A tensor_C V_{1/2}^C as complex A-modules.

    Proof attempt: Define the map
    phi: A tensor_C V_{1/2}^C -> A tensor_R V_{1/2}
    phi(a tensor_C (v tensor_R z)) = (za) tensor_R v

    And the inverse:
    psi: A tensor_R V_{1/2} -> A tensor_C V_{1/2}^C
    psi(a tensor_R v) = a tensor_C (v tensor_R 1)

    Check: phi is well-defined over C (i.e., phi(a tensor_C z(v tensor 1)) = phi(a tensor_C (v tensor z)) = (za) tensor v; and phi(za tensor_C (v tensor 1)) = (za) tensor v). These agree, so phi is well-defined.

    Check: psi(za tensor v) = za tensor_C (v tensor 1) = a tensor_C z(v tensor 1) = a tensor_C (v tensor z). And phi(psi(a tensor v)) = phi(a tensor_C (v tensor 1)) = a tensor v. So phi and psi are mutually inverse.

    4. WHAT THIS MEANS: The left A-module A tensor_R V_{1/2} is CANONICALLY isomorphic to A tensor_C V_{1/2}^C. The complexification V_{1/2}^C appears NECESSARILY when we form the observer-target tensor product and demand it be a left A-module.

    5. BUT: is this the RIGHT model for "observer probes V_{1/2}"? The tensor product A tensor V_{1/2} models "observer has a copy of each V_{1/2} element for each of its own degrees of freedom." Is this physically correct? The alternative is that the observer probes V_{1/2} through the PEIRCE INTERFACE (V_1 * V_{1/2} subset V_{1/2}), which is a much more constrained interaction than a full tensor product.

    6. HANCHE-OLSEN CONTEXT: Hanche-Olsen's work on JC-algebra tensor products (1983) establishes that for JC-algebras B and C, the tensor product B tensor C can be defined within the Jordan framework. But V_{1/2} is NOT a Jordan algebra; it is a Jordan module. And h_3(O) is NOT a JC-algebra. Analyze what Hanche-Olsen's framework says (or doesn't say) about this situation.

    Specifically: Hanche-Olsen shows that every JC-algebra B has a "universal C*-algebra" C*(B) such that B embeds in C*(B)^sa and the Jordan structure is recovered. For h_3(O), the non-associativity means no such embedding exists (Alfsen-Shultz). But for the PURPOSE OF FORMING A tensor V_{1/2}, we only need V_{1/2} to be a real vector space, not a Jordan algebra. The tensor product A tensor_R V (for A a C*-algebra, V a real vector space) is always well-defined.

    ASSERT_CONVENTION at top: jordan_product=(1/2)(ab+ba), peirce_decomposition=under_E11, tensor_R=tensor_over_reals
  </action>

    3. The canonical isomorphism phi/psi is well-defined and verified to be mutually inverse

  <name>Task 2: Determine whether tensor product route forces canonical complexification</name>
  <files>derivations/15-route4-tensor-product.md</files>
  <action>
    Resolve the decisive question for Route 4: does the tensor product model force a CANONICAL complexification of V_{1/2}?

    The situation from Task 1: A tensor_R V_{1/2} = A tensor_C V_{1/2}^C canonically. This is a mathematical tautology: for any C-algebra A and real vector space V, A tensor_R V = A tensor_C (V tensor_R C). The complexification V^C = V tensor_R C emerges automatically.

    But the question is more subtle: does V_{1/2} itself (not just A tensor V_{1/2}) acquire a canonical complex structure from the observer?

    Three levels of the answer:

    LEVEL 1 (algebraic tautology): A tensor_R V = A tensor_C V^C always. This proves that V^C appears in the tensor product. But it does NOT prove that V itself carries a complex structure. The complexification lives in the tensor product, not in V alone.

    LEVEL 2 (Peirce-enhanced): The observer doesn't just tensor with V_{1/2}. It interacts through the Peirce interface: V_1 * V_{1/2} subset V_{1/2}. Since V_1 = R * E_11, the observer "enriches" V_1 from R to C (because M_n(C)^sa contains C*E_11 as a subalgebra). The enriched Peirce rule becomes: C * V_{1/2} subset V_{1/2}^? -- but what is V_{1/2}^? ?

    If the Peirce multiplication L_a: V_{1/2} -> V_{1/2} for a in V_1 extends C-linearly when we upgrade V_1 from R to C, then we need L_{ia}: V_{1/2} -> V_{1/2} to exist. Define J = L_{i*E_11} (multiplication by i*E_11 on V_{1/2}). For this to define a complex structure, we need:
    - J: V_{1/2} -> V_{1/2} (well-defined)
    - J^2 = -Id (complex structure condition)

    But i*E_11 is NOT in h_3(O) (it is not Hermitian). It IS in h_3(O)^C = h_3(O) tensor_R C. So J is defined on V_{1/2}^C, not on V_{1/2} itself. This is again the extension-of-scalars argument, now seen from the tensor product angle.

    LEVEL 3 (physical interpretation): The observer's measurement on V_{1/2} involves forming correlators like omega(a tensor v) for states omega on A tensor V_{1/2}. Since A tensor_R V_{1/2} = A tensor_C V_{1/2}^C, the observer's description of V_{1/2} is automatically complexified. The observer CANNOT probe V_{1/2} as a real space because its algebra's C-linearity forces the tensor product to contain V_{1/2}^C.

    SYNTHESIS: The tensor product route gives a POSITIVE result, but it is essentially the extension-of-scalars argument from Phase 18 restated in tensor language. The result is:

    THEOREM (Route 4): Let A = M_n(C) be the observer's C*-algebra and V_{1/2} = O^2 the Peirce half-space. The space of "observer-target" configurations A tensor_R V_{1/2} is canonically isomorphic to A tensor_C V_{1/2}^C, where V_{1/2}^C = V_{1/2} tensor_R C. In particular:
    (a) V_{1/2}^C appears necessarily in the observer's description
    (b) The complex structure on V_{1/2}^C is canonical (determined by A's C-algebra structure)
    (c) The isomorphism is natural (functorial in V_{1/2})

    HOWEVER: this is a WEAK result. It says the complexification appears in the tensor product, not that V_{1/2} itself carries a complex structure. The complexification is "in the observer's eye," not "in the world."

    State whether this constitutes a theorem (complexification forced) or an obstruction (complexification not forced on V_{1/2} itself). Address the distinction between "observer sees V_{1/2}^C" and "V_{1/2} IS complex."

    Also analyze: is this specific to h_3(O), or does it work for ANY real vector space V tensored with a C*-algebra? If the latter, it does not use the Jordan/Peirce structure and may be a forbidden proxy (too generic).
  </action>
