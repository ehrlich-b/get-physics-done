    **Part I: The F_4 intersection route (Todorov-Drenska)**

    Step 1: State the starting point. F_4 = Aut(h_3(O)) with dim = 52. The observer selects E_11 (rank-1 idempotent), giving Spin(9) = Stab_{F_4}(E_11) with dim = 36 (Phase 18).

    Step 2: The same complex structure u = e_7 that was used in Phase 19 to define Cl(6) also acts on Im(O) via left multiplication, giving the automorphism structure of O. The stabilizer of u in G_2 = Aut(O) is SU(3), the color group:
      - G_2 subset F_4 (automorphisms of O extend to automorphisms of h_3(O))
      - dim(G_2) = 14, dim(SU(3)) = 8
      - S^6 = G_2/SU(3) (the orbit of any unit imaginary octonion under G_2)

    Step 3: The choice of u breaks F_4. Todorov-Drenska (arXiv:1805.06739): choosing u in Im(O) preserves the subgroup of F_4 that commutes with left multiplication by u. This gives:
      - Stab_{F_4}(u) superset [SU(3) x SU(3)]/Z_3
      - One SU(3) is SU(3)_C = Stab_{G_2}(u) (color)
      - The other SU(3) is a "flavor" SU(3) from the h_2(O) structure
      - Dimension check: 8 + 8 = 16 (or more precisely track through the F_4 root system)

    Step 4: The SM gauge group from intersection. Compute:
      Stab_{F_4}(E_11) cap Stab_{F_4}(u) = Spin(9) cap Stab_{F_4}(u)

    This intersection gives SU(3)_C x SU(2) x U(1) with dim = 12 = 8 + 3 + 1. The SU(2) comes from the Spin(4) part of Spin(9), and U(1) from the residual structure. This is the Todorov-Drenska result: the SM gauge group from F_4 subgroup intersection.

    Cross-check: dim(F_4) = 52, codim(SM) = 52 - 12 = 40. The 40 broken generators correspond to the coset space.

    NOTE: The F_4 route gives the gauge GROUP but does NOT specify a chiral representation. The 27 of h_3(O) does not naturally carry a chiral decomposition.
