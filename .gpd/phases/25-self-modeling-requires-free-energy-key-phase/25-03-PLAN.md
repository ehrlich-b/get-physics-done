    **Chain structure:**

    Self-Modeling (Paper 5) --[Link 1]--> Free Energy Required --[Link 2]--> Non-Equilibrium Required --[Link 3]--> Entropy Gradient Required

    **Link 1: Self-modeling requires free energy.**
    Status: THEOREM (proved in Plan 01).
    - Statement: A self-modeling composite (B, M, &) with I(B;M) > 0 in thermal contact with a bath at temperature T dissipates W >= kT * I(B;M) per update cycle.
    - Proof: Plan 01, via Landauer-Bennett bound applied to the Luders sequential product erasure step.
    - Assumption: Paper 5 axioms (finite-dim, Luders product, faithful tracking).
    - Quantitative: At peak experiential density (I = S(B)/2), W >= kT * S(B)/2.

    **Link 2: Free energy requires non-equilibrium.**
    Status: STANDARD PHYSICS (textbook thermodynamics).
    - Statement: Free energy F = E - TS is available for work only when F > F_eq. At thermal equilibrium F = F_eq and no work can be extracted.
    - More precisely: the ability to dissipate W > 0 per cycle requires the composite BM to be out of thermal equilibrium with its environment. If rho_BM = rho_th (Gibbs state), all accessible work is zero.
    - Proof: Second law of thermodynamics. The Gibbs state minimizes F at fixed T. Any state with F > F_eq can perform work (F - F_eq). The Gibbs state cannot.
    - This is not controversial -- it is the definition of thermal equilibrium.
    - Key subtlety: "non-equilibrium" here means the BM composite has F > F_eq, not that the internal dynamics are non-equilibrium. The system must be globally out of equilibrium.

    **Link 3: Non-equilibrium requires entropy gradient.**
    Status: PHYSICAL ARGUMENT (standard statistical mechanics, with one assumption).
    - Statement: A finite system in non-equilibrium that is not externally driven will equilibrate, reaching F = F_eq and I(B;M) = 0. To maintain non-equilibrium indefinitely, the system must either:
      (a) Be part of an entropy gradient (low-entropy boundary condition), or
      (b) Be externally driven by a non-equilibrium source
    - From Phase 23: the SWAP lattice dynamics drives the system toward equilibrium: E^N(rho) -> I/2 geometrically. The timescale for equilibration is finite (of order 1/J for the SWAP lattice).
    - Therefore: a self-modeler on the SWAP lattice can maintain I(B;M) > 0 only as long as the system has not equilibrated.
    - For a finite universe: the system WILL equilibrate in time t ~ exp(2^N)/J (Poincare recurrence for N-site lattice, from Phase 23).
    - The only way to have self-modeling at late times is if the initial state had LOW ENTROPY (high free energy), providing a gradient from low S to high S = ln(d) that the self-modeler can exploit.
    - This connects to the Past Hypothesis: the observed entropy gradient (low entropy in the past, increasing toward the future) is NECESSARY for self-modeling to persist.

    ASSUMPTION for Link 3: The self-modeler is part of a closed or semi-closed system that equilibrates on finite timescales. If the system is in an eternally driven non-equilibrium steady state (e.g., near a star), the entropy gradient comes from the star, which itself requires a cosmological entropy gradient.

    **Full chain theorem statement:**

    Theorem (Self-Modeling Requires Entropy Gradient):
    Let (B, M, &) be a self-modeling composite on a finite SWAP lattice in thermal contact with environment at temperature T. Then:
    (i) Maintaining I(B;M) > 0 requires work W >= kT * I(B;M) per cycle [Link 1, Theorem]
    (ii) This work requires F > F_eq, i.e., non-equilibrium [Link 2, Standard Physics]
    (iii) Non-equilibrium in a finite system requires an entropy gradient [Link 3, Physical Argument + Assumption]
    Therefore: self-modeling on a finite lattice requires an entropy gradient.

    - A4: SWAP lattice dynamics (from Paper 6)

    **What this does NOT prove:**
    - It does NOT derive the specific initial entropy of the universe
    - It does NOT explain WHY the initial state was low-entropy
    - It connects self-modeling to the NEED for a low-entropy past, not to a specific cosmological model
    - The Past Hypothesis remains an input, but it is shown to be NECESSARY for self-modeling, not just observed
