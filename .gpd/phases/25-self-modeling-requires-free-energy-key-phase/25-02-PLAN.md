  <name>Task 1: Analyze the coherence loophole and derive resolution</name>
  <files>derivations/25-coherence-loophole.md</files>
  <action>
    Analyze whether quantum coherence can bypass the Landauer bound for self-modeling.

    **Step 1: State the loophole precisely.**
    The coherence loophole: In quantum mechanics, coherence is a resource. A system in a coherent superposition can perform information processing without the thermodynamic cost of classical measurement + erasure. Could a self-modeler exploit coherence to maintain I(B;M) > 0 without free energy?

    Specifically: if the self-modeling update is implemented unitarily (no measurement, no decoherence), then no information is erased, and the Landauer bound does not apply directly.

    **Step 2: Show why the Luders product closes the loophole.**
    The self-modeling sequential product from Paper 5 is a & b = sqrt(a) b sqrt(a).
    This is a Luders-type operation. Key properties:
    (a) It is a CPTP map (completely positive, trace-preserving) -- proven in Phase 23
    (b) The Luders product involves the square root of an effect a, which acts as a measurement
    (c) After the Luders product, the state of B is projected into the eigenbasis of a
    (d) This DESTROYS coherence between eigenstates of a with different eigenvalues
    (e) The coherence destruction is information loss -- it requires erasure in subsequent cycles
    (f) Therefore the Landauer bound applies to each cycle

    **Step 3: Address the unitary evasion attempt.**
    Could the self-modeler avoid the Luders product by using unitary evolution only?
    - Paper 5 REQUIRES the Luders sequential product -- it is the DEFINING operation of the self-modeling framework
    - The sequential product axioms S1-S7 (proved in Phase 4) characterize exactly this map
    - A unitary-only protocol would NOT satisfy the self-modeling axioms (specifically, S2 fails for unitary evolution of the composite: the sequential product a & b is NOT the same as unitary evolution U rho U^dag)
    - Therefore: within the Paper 5 framework, the self-modeling update IS the Luders product, and coherence IS destroyed at each step

    **Step 4: Show that maintaining coherence itself costs free energy.**
    Even if a generalized framework could avoid the Luders product:
    (a) Coherence in a thermal bath decays on the decoherence timescale t_d
    (b) Maintaining coherence against thermal decoherence requires active error correction
    (c) Active error correction requires work (Landauer bound on syndrome erasure)
    (d) Therefore maintaining coherence in a thermal environment IS a non-equilibrium process
    (e) A system at TRUE thermal equilibrium has zero coherence (diagonal density matrix in energy basis)

    This is a SECOND, independent argument: even without invoking the Luders product specifically, maintaining quantum coherence in a thermal bath is itself a non-equilibrium resource that requires free energy.

    **Step 5: Sagawa-Ueda cross-check (VALD-02).**
    The Sagawa-Ueda framework treats measurement and feedback control:
    - System measured, yielding outcome y with probability p(y)
    - Feedback: controller applies unitary U_y depending on outcome
    - Generalized Jarzynski equality: <exp(-beta(W - Delta_F))> = exp(I_fc)
      where I_fc = sum_y p(y) ln(p(y|x_f)/p(y)) is the transfer entropy

    Map the self-modeling cycle onto this:
    - System = body B
    - Controller = model M
    - Measurement = the Luders product tests B
    - Feedback = M is updated based on the outcome
    - Transfer entropy I_fc relates to I(B;M)

    Show:
    - The Sagawa-Ueda mean inequality gives: <W> >= Delta_F - kT * I_fc
    - For a cyclic process (Delta_F = 0): <W> >= -kT * I_fc, or equivalently W_dissipated >= kT * I_fc
    - This is CONSISTENT with our bound W >= kT * I(B;M) since I_fc <= I(B;M) in general
    - The Sagawa-Ueda framework may give a tighter bound (I_fc vs I(B;M))
    - No contradiction: our bound is a consequence of theirs

    **Step 6: State the resolution clearly.**
    Resolution: The coherence loophole is CLOSED within the Paper 5 framework because:
    (R1) The self-modeling sequential product IS a CPTP map that destroys coherence (framework-specific)
    (R2) Maintaining coherence in a thermal bath itself requires free energy (framework-independent)
    (R3) The Sagawa-Ueda fluctuation theorem independently confirms the Landauer bound applies to feedback controllers (which is what a self-modeler is)

    State which parts are theorems, which are standard physics arguments, and which are assumptions.
    Note: the resolution depends on Paper 5's specific axioms. A non-Luders self-modeling framework is an acknowledged uncertainty.

    1. **Construct the optimal coherent protocol.**
       For qubit B and qubit M:
       - Initial state: rho_BM = |psi><psi| with |psi> = cos(theta)|00> + sin(theta)|11> (variable entanglement)
       - This gives I(B;M) = h(cos^2(theta)) where h is binary entropy (in nats)
       - The coherence is C = |<00|rho_BM|11>| = |sin(theta) cos(theta)| = sin(2*theta)/2

    2. **Apply the Luders sequential product and measure coherence loss.**
       - Implement a & b = sqrt(a) b sqrt(a) for the self-modeling update
       - Choose a = projection onto tracking accuracy (effect from Paper 5)
       - Compute the coherence before and after the Luders product
       - Verify: coherence in the eigenbasis of a is destroyed (reduced by measurement backaction)

    3. **Compute work cost of the full cycle.**
       - For the Luders protocol: W_Luders >= kT * I(B;M) from Plan 01
       - For a hypothetical unitary protocol: compute I(B;M) achievable unitarily
       - Compare: even the unitary protocol cannot maintain I(B;M) > 0 in a thermal bath without work

    4. **Sagawa-Ueda bound computation.**

       - Compute the Sagawa-Ueda bound: W >= -kT * I_fc (for cyclic process)
       - Compare with our bound: W >= kT * I(B;M)
       - Verify: our bound is consistent (I(B;M) >= I_fc in general, so our bound may be weaker)

    5. **Exhaustive coherence test.**
       - 50 random initial entangled states |psi> for B+M
       - For each: compute I(B;M) before/after Luders product
       - Compute coherence before/after
