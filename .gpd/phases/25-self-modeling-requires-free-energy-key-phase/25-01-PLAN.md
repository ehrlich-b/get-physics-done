  <action>
    Map the self-modeling cycle to an information-processing cycle and derive the Landauer bound.

    **Step 1: Define the self-modeling cycle explicitly.**
    From Paper 5, self-modeling consists of:
    - Body B in state rho_B (the physical system)
    - Model M in state rho_M (the internal representation)
    - Faithful tracking: M tracks B, meaning I(B;M) > 0 measures self-modeling quality
    - The sequential product a & b = sqrt(a) b sqrt(a) implements the update

    The self-modeling update cycle is:
    (i) Test: Measure/interact B to extract information
    (ii) Update: Write new information into M (this overwrites old M state)
    (iii) Test again: Verify tracking accuracy

    **Step 2: Identify the erasure step.**
    Following Bennett's resolution of Maxwell's demon:
    - The model M must be RESET before each update (or the old model state must be erased)
    - This erasure is logically irreversible: many possible old M states map to one reset state
    - By Landauer's principle, erasing one bit costs at least kT ln 2 of heat dissipation
    - For quantum systems, the generalization (Reeb-Wolf 2014): erasing a quantum state with entropy S costs at least kT * S in work

    **Step 3: Derive the quantitative bound.**
    The information that must be erased per cycle is the mutual information I(B;M):
    - Before update: M contains I_old bits of information about B
    - The update overwrites M with new tracking data
    - The old tracking data (I_old nats) must be erased
    - Minimum work per cycle: W >= kT * I(B;M)

    Express this in terms of the experiential density:
    - rho = I(B;M) * (1 - I(B;M)/S(B))
    - At the peak rho_max (I = S(B)/2): W >= kT * S(B)/2
    - Higher mutual information = more free energy required

    **Step 4: Verify the equilibrium limit (VALD-03).**
    At thermal equilibrium, rho_BM = (1/d) * I (maximally mixed):
    - S(B) = ln(d_B), S(M) = ln(d_M), S(BM) = ln(d_B * d_M) = ln(d_B) + ln(d_M)
    - I(B;M) = S(B) + S(M) - S(BM) = 0
    - Therefore rho = I * (1 - I/S(B)) = 0
    - The Landauer bound gives W >= 0 (trivially satisfied)
    - Self-modeling is impossible: no information about B is stored in M
    - This is the rho = 0 result needed for Phase 26

    **Step 5: Connect to the SWAP lattice dynamics from Phase 23.**
    From Phase 23, Plan 01:
    - The CPTP channel E(rho_B) = cos^2(Jt) rho_B + sin^2(Jt) I/2
    - After N interactions with fresh I/2 bath: E^N(rho) -> I/2 (thermal equilibrium)
    - Therefore I(B;M) -> 0 as the system equilibrates
    - Self-modeling degrades as the system approaches equilibrium
    - The self-modeler must RESIST equilibration to maintain I(B;M) > 0
    - Resisting equilibration requires free energy (maintaining a non-equilibrium state)

    **Step 6: State the result as a theorem.**
    Theorem (Landauer bound on self-modeling):
    Let (B, M, &) be a self-modeling composite in the sense of Paper 5, with B and M
    finite-dimensional quantum systems in thermal contact with a heat bath at temperature T.
    Then:
    (a) Each self-modeling update cycle dissipates work W >= kT * I(B;M)
    (b) At thermal equilibrium (rho_BM maximally mixed), I(B;M) = 0 and rho = 0
    (c) Maintaining I(B;M) > 0 requires the composite to be out of thermal equilibrium

    State all assumptions explicitly. Mark which steps are theorems vs. physical arguments.

    Reference Landauer 1961 for the classical bound, Bennett 1982 for the Maxwell's demon connection, and Reeb-Wolf 2014 for the quantum generalization.
