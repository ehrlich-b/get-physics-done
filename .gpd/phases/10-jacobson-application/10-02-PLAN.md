    **Part A: Setting (Jacobson Step 1).**

    Consider a point p in the emergent spacetime manifold. Choose coordinates so g_ab(p) = eta_ab (locally flat). Consider a small geodesic ball B of radius R centered at p, lying in a spacelike hypersurface. The causal diamond D(B) is the domain of dependence of B.

    **Part B: Entropy decomposition (Jacobson Step 2).**

    S = S_UV + S_mat

    where:
    - S_UV = eta * A (area-proportional, UV-divergent in continuum, finite on lattice)
    - S_mat = finite, state-dependent, matter contribution

    Under a first-order perturbation away from the maximally symmetric spacetime (MSS) vacuum:

    delta S = delta S_UV + delta S_mat

    Note: in the MSS vacuum, all curvature invariants are determined by the cosmological constant. A perturbation away from MSS introduces inhomogeneous curvature and/or matter.

    d(theta)/d(lambda) = -(1/(d-1)) theta^2 - sigma_{ab} sigma^{ab} - R_{ab} k^a k^b

    theta^{(1)}(lambda) = -integral_0^lambda R_{ab}^{(1)} k^a k^b d lambda'
