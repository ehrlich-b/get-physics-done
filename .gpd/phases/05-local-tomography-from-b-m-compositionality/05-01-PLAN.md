  <files>derivations/05-local-tomography.md, code/sp_verification.py</files>
  <action>
    Define the composite order unit space V_BM and its product-form sequential product. This must use ONLY OUS/GPT primitives -- no Hilbert spaces, no complex structure.

    **Step 1: Define the composite state space S(V_BM).**

    Following Plavala (arXiv:2103.07469) and Barnum-Wilce (arXiv:1202.4513):
    - V_B and V_M are both order unit spaces with sequential products satisfying S1-S7 (established in Phase 4).
    - V_B and V_M are the SAME OUS (self-modeling: M models B, so they share the same algebraic structure). For qubits, V_B = V_M = V_3 (spin factor, = M_2(C)^sa).
    - The composite V_BM is an order unit space whose state space S(V_BM) is a compact convex set satisfying:
      (a) S(V_BM) contains all product states: omega_B tensor omega_M for all states omega_B in S(V_B), omega_M in S(V_M).
      (b) Non-signaling: for every joint state omega in S(V_BM), the marginals omega_B(a) = omega(a tensor 1_M) and omega_M(b) = omega(1_B tensor b) are well-defined states on V_B and V_M respectively.
      (c) Effect space: E(V_BM) contains all product effects a_B tensor b_M, where (a_B tensor b_M)(omega) = omega(a_B tensor b_M) for product states extends bilinearly.

    **Step 2: Define "independently accessible."**

    Formalize the operational content of "B and M are independently accessible in the self-modeling composite":
    - For every effect a on B and every effect b on M, the product effect a tensor b is a valid effect on V_BM.
    - For every state preparation on B alone and every state preparation on M alone, the product preparation is a valid preparation on V_BM.
    - Non-signaling holds: measuring B does not disturb M's marginal, and vice versa.

    This is the MINIMAL composite -- it says B and M can be probed independently but does NOT (yet) specify the dimension of S(V_BM).

    **Step 3: Define the product-form sequential product on V_BM.**

    For product effects:
    (a_B tensor b_M) & (c_B tensor d_M) = (a_B & c_B) tensor (b_M & d_M)

    where & on the right uses the Phase 4 corrected product (Eq. 04-06.4) on each factor. Extend to all effects on V_BM by the bilinear structure of the composite effect space.

    **Step 4: Verify S1-S7 inheritance on the composite.**

    Show that the product-form SP on V_BM satisfies S1-S7, given that the factor SPs satisfy S1-S7 (Phase 4):
    - S1 (additivity in 2nd arg): (a tensor b) & ((c tensor d) + (e tensor f)) = (a tensor b) & (c tensor d) + (a tensor b) & (e tensor f). Follows from factor S1.
    - S2 (continuity in 1st arg): If a_n tensor b_n -> a tensor b, then (a_n tensor b_n) & (c tensor d) -> (a tensor b) & (c tensor d). Follows from factor S2.
    - S3 (unitality): (1_B tensor 1_M) & (c tensor d) = c tensor d. Follows from factor S3.
    - S4 (orthogonality symmetry): If (a tensor b) & (c tensor d) = 0, then (c tensor d) & (a tensor b) = 0. Since (a tensor b) & (c tensor d) = (a & c) tensor (b & d) = 0 iff a & c = 0 or b & d = 0 (in an OUS where the product of nonzero effects can be zero only if one factor is zero -- VERIFY THIS CLAIM CAREFULLY for general effects, not just sharp ones). By factor S4, the reverse product also vanishes.
    - S5-S7 (compatibility axioms): Extend from factors similarly.

    IMPORTANT for S4: The claim "(a & c) tensor (b & d) = 0 iff a & c = 0 or b & d = 0" needs careful justification. In an OUS, the tensor product of two nonzero positive elements is nonzero (product states are nonzero). But effects can have zero product without either being zero (that is what orthogonality means). The correct argument: (a & c) tensor (b & d) = 0 as an element of V_BM means it acts as zero on all states. For product states omega_B tensor omega_M: omega_B(a & c) * omega_M(b & d) = 0 for all omega_B, omega_M. This means omega_B(a & c) = 0 for all omega_B (i.e., a & c = 0) OR omega_M(b & d) = 0 for all omega_M (i.e., b & d = 0). Then factor S4 gives the reverse.

    **Step 5: SymPy numerical verification.**

    Extend sp_verification.py to verify:
    - Product-form SP on V_3 tensor V_3 (= M_2(C)^sa tensor M_2(C)^sa): construct concrete 4x4 product effects, compute composite SP, verify S1-S7 hold numerically.
    - Dimension check: dim(V_3) = 4; verify that the composite effect space has the right structure.
    - Classical limit: composite of 2-simplices should give pointwise product on product space.

    Do NOT use complex structure in the composite construction. The composite V_BM is defined as a real vector space with OUS structure. Complex structure emerges only at the end of Phase 5.

    CIRCULARITY AUDIT at the end of the derivation: verify zero Hilbert space imports in the composite construction. Hilbert spaces, complex numbers, and C*-algebras may appear ONLY in:
    (a) The SymPy harness (concrete M_2(C)^sa examples for numerical verification).
    (b) The statement of the conclusion (not the proof).

  <name>Task 2: Prove faithful tracking implies local tomography</name>
  <files>derivations/05-local-tomography.md</files>
  <action>
    This is THE central novel argument. Prove that faithful B-M self-modeling forces the composite state space to satisfy the local tomography condition: dim(S(V_BM)) = dim(S(V_B)) * dim(S(V_M)).

    **The argument structure (from research, Approach 1, Step 4):**

    **Lower bound (product states exist):**
    The non-signaling composite V_BM contains all product states omega_B tensor omega_M. The affine span of product states has dimension >= dim(S(V_B)) * dim(S(V_M)) (standard GPT result; cite Barnum-Wilce). Therefore:
    dim(S(V_BM)) >= dim(S(V_B)) * dim(S(V_M))

    **Upper bound (faithful tracking constrains the composite):**
    The self-modeling constraint provides a SPECIFIC joint state rho_BM: the self-modeling correlation state, which encodes M's tracking of B. Faithful tracking means phi: V_B -> V_M is an isomorphism (established in Phase 4: phi being an isomorphism selects f = sqrt, recovering Luders).

    The key claim: faithful tracking means the B-M correlation state rho_BM SEPARATES product effects. Formally:
    - For any product effect a_B tensor b_M, define its expectation: E[a_B tensor b_M] = rho_BM(a_B tensor b_M).
    - Faithful tracking means: if E[a_B tensor b_M] = E[a_B tensor b_M'] for all a_B, then b_M = b_M' (M's effects are distinguished by their correlations with B). And symmetrically for B's effects.
    - More precisely: the bilinear map (a_B, b_M) -> rho_BM(a_B tensor b_M) is non-degenerate.

    Non-degeneracy of the correlation state means: the state space S(V_BM) is spanned by product effects up to the non-signaling constraints. Any joint state omega in S(V_BM) is determined by its values on product effects {a_B tensor b_M}. Therefore:
    dim(S(V_BM)) <= dim(S(V_B)) * dim(S(V_M))

    **Combining:** dim(S(V_BM)) = dim(S(V_B)) * dim(S(V_M)). This IS local tomography.

    **CRITICAL: Address the entangled sector explicitly.**

    In real QM (M_n(R)^sa), the correlation state does NOT separate product effects because dim(S(V_R tensor V_R)) > dim(S(V_R))^2 -- there exist "entangled" states invisible to product measurements. The self-modeling constraint must provide the additional structure that prevents this.

    The argument why faithful tracking closes the gap:
    - A faithful self-model means phi: V_B -> V_M is an ORDER isomorphism (not just a linear map -- it preserves the positive cone and the order unit).
    - The correlation state rho_BM is constructed from phi: for a product state omega_B, the corresponding model state is phi_*(omega_B), giving a joint state rho_BM that "entangles" B and M maximally subject to the self-modeling constraint.
    - For this maximally correlated state, the bilinear form (a, b) -> rho_BM(a tensor b) = omega_B(a * phi^{-1}(b)) (or similar -- derive the exact form) is non-degenerate because phi is an isomorphism and omega_B can be chosen to separate effects.
    - Concretely: choose omega_B = maximally mixed state (trace state). Then rho_BM(a tensor b) = Tr(a * phi^{-1}(b)) / Tr(1), which is non-degenerate because the trace inner product is non-degenerate on the EJA.

    BUT WAIT -- does this require the trace inner product, which uses Jordan algebra structure? Phase 4 established that the OUS is an EJA, so the trace form IS available. The question is whether using it is circular. It is NOT circular because:
    - Phase 4 derived the EJA structure from S1-S7 via vdW Theorem 1.
    - The trace form on the EJA is an intrinsic property of the EJA, not an import from complex QM.
    - We are using the EJA structure (Phase 4 output) to DERIVE local tomography (Phase 5 output). The logical order is: self-modeling -> S1-S7 -> EJA -> local tomography -> C*-algebra.

    **Verify the argument does NOT work for real QM:**
    For M_2(R)^sa (3-dimensional, symmetric 2x2 real matrices): dim(V_R) = 3. If we form the composite and apply the same argument, we get dim(S) should = 9. But the actual composite of M_2(R)^sa has dim(S) > 9 because there exist entangled states not accessible to product measurements. The resolution: M_2(R)^sa does NOT arise as a self-modeling system in our framework because Phase 4's positivity bound + faithful tracking selects f = sqrt, which gives the Luders product, which lives on M_2(C)^sa (= V_3), not on M_2(R)^sa (which would be V_2). The type selection happened in Phase 4, and the local tomography argument confirms it.

    Alternatively, if we try to apply the argument abstractly to M_2(R)^sa: the trace form on the real Jordan algebra M_2(R)^sa IS non-degenerate (it is an EJA). But the composite state space of M_2(R)^sa is NOT determined by the trace form alone -- there are additional entangled states. The difference is that for the COMPLEX type, the product state space exhausts the full composite (dim matches), while for the real type it does not. The key fact: dim(M_n(C)^sa tensor M_m(C)^sa) = n^2 * m^2 = dim(M_n(C)^sa) * dim(M_m(C)^sa), but dim(M_n(R)^sa tensor M_m(R)^sa) as an OUS composite exceeds dim(M_n(R)^sa) * dim(M_m(R)^sa) because the real tensor product introduces extra degrees of freedom (cf. Hardy 2001, Barnum-Wilce 2014).

    The self-modeling argument must identify WHERE the complex type enters: it is through Phase 4's selection of V_3 (not V_2 or other types). The local tomography proof then confirms that V_3 = M_2(C)^sa is the only EJA type for which the product state space exhausts the composite. This is not a circular argument but a CONSISTENCY CHECK: Phase 4 selects the type, Phase 5 confirms that only the selected type admits local tomography.

    Write the complete proof in derivations/05-local-tomography.md, continuing the document from Task 1.
