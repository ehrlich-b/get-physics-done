  <files>derivations/05-type-exclusion-and-cstar.md, code/sp_verification.py</files>
  <action>
    Systematically exclude each non-complex EJA type from the JVW classification. For each type, provide an explicit argument why it cannot arise in a locally tomographic self-modeling composite.

    **Type 1: Real matrices M_n(R)^sa**

    dim(M_n(R)^sa) = n(n+1)/2.
    For two copies: dim(M_n(R)^sa)^2 = [n(n+1)/2]^2.
    But the minimal composite state space of M_n(R)^sa tensor M_n(R)^sa has dimension GREATER than [n(n+1)/2]^2 (Hardy 2001, Barnum-Wilce 2014). There exist entangled states invisible to product measurements.

    For n=2: dim = 3, dim^2 = 9, but composite dim > 9. Local tomography FAILS.

    The "extra" dimensions come from the symmetric tensor product structure of real matrices, which has additional antisymmetric entangled states not accessible to product measurements.

    **Type 2: Quaternionic matrices M_n(H)^sa**

    dim(M_n(H)^sa) = n(2n-1).
    For two copies: dim(M_n(H)^sa)^2 = [n(2n-1)]^2.
    The minimal composite state space has dimension LESS than [n(2n-1)]^2. This is established in Barnum-Wilce (arXiv:1202.4513, Section IV) and the dimension counting table in Barnum-Graydon-Wilce (arXiv:1606.09331, Table 1). Product measurements over-determine the joint state.

    For n=2: dim = 10, dim^2 = 100, but composite dim < 100. Local tomography FAILS.

    The "missing" dimensions come from quaternionic anti-commutation constraints that reduce the effective product state space. The quaternionic tensor product has fewer independent real parameters than the square of the factor dimensions because the quaternionic structure imposes additional symplectic constraints on the composite (Barnum-Wilce 2014, Section IV.B).

    **Type 3: Spin factors V_n for n >= 4**

    dim(V_n) = n + 1.
    For V_3 (= M_2(C)^sa, our qubit): dim = 4, dim^2 = 16. Local tomography HOLDS (this is complex QM).
    For V_4: dim = 5, dim^2 = 25. But V_4 admits no locally tomographic composite (Barnum-Wilce). The spin factor with n >= 4 is NOT isomorphic to any M_k(C)^sa.

    Note: V_3 = M_2(C)^sa is the ONLY spin factor compatible with local tomography. V_2 = M_2(R)^sa (real qubit, excluded above). V_n for n >= 4 are excluded here.

    **Type 4: Albert algebra M_3(O)^sa**

    dim(M_3(O)^sa) = 27.
    The Albert algebra admits NO non-signaling composite AT ALL (Barnum-Graydon-Wilce, Quantum 4, 359, 2020). Not just "no locally tomographic composite" -- NO composite period. This is the strongest exclusion.

    Cite BGW Theorem specifically. Verify hypothesis: the composite must be non-signaling and include product states. The Albert algebra's exceptional structure (octonion non-associativity) prevents constructing a well-behaved tensor product. Also consult Barnum-Ududec-van de Wetering (arXiv:2306.00362) for additional compositionality constraints on Jordan algebras that may simplify the geometric assumptions in the exclusion argument.

    **Type 5: Complex matrices M_n(C)^sa (PASSES)**

    dim(M_n(C)^sa) = n^2.
    For two copies: dim^2 = n^4.
    Composite state space: dim(M_n(C)^sa tensor M_m(C)^sa) = n^2 * m^2 = dim * dim. Local tomography HOLDS.

    This is the ONLY type for which dim(composite) = dim(factor) * dim(factor). It is the Goldilocks condition.

    **Construct the dimension table in the derivation.** Include the table from the research file (both general n and specific n=2).

    **SymPy verification:** Add code to sp_verification.py that:
    - Computes dim(M_n(K)^sa) for K = R, C, H and n = 2, 3, 4.
    - Computes dim^2 for each.
    - Reports whether local tomography would hold (dim matches) for each type.
    - Confirms V_3 = 4 = dim(M_2(C)^sa).
  </action>

    **Step 1: Invoke van de Wetering Theorem 3 (arXiv:1803.11139, p.19).**

    Statement: If V is a sequential product space (satisfying S1-S7) and V tensor V with the product-form sequential product is also a sequential product space with a locally tomographic composite, then V is (the self-adjoint part of) a C*-algebra.

    Hypothesis verification:

    - V tensor V with product-form SP satisfies S1-S7: proved in Plan 01, Task 1.
    - Composite is locally tomographic: proved in Plan 01, Task 2.

    Conclusion: V is the self-adjoint part of a C*-algebra.

    **Step 2: Invoke Barnum-Wilce (arXiv:1202.4513, Theorem).**

    Statement: If V is a finite-dimensional EJA that admits a locally tomographic composite and contains a qubit subsystem (V_3 = M_2(C)^sa), then V = M_n(C)^sa for some n.

    Hypothesis verification:
    - V is a finite-dimensional EJA: Phase 4 + vdW Theorem 1.
    - V admits a locally tomographic composite: Plan 01.
    - V contains a qubit subsystem: Phase 4 established V_3 = M_2(C)^sa for the qubit self-model. For general n, the self-modeling framework always contains a 2-level subsystem (any pair of orthogonal sharp effects defines a qubit-like subsystem).

    Conclusion: V = M_n(C)^sa.

    **Step 3: Invoke Hanche-Olsen (LNM 1132, 1985).**

    Statement: If V is a JB-algebra that admits a tensor product (in the sense that V tensor V is again a JB-algebra with product states), then V is the self-adjoint part of a C*-algebra.

    This is consistent with and reinforces Steps 1-2. Hanche-Olsen provides the original Jordan-to-C* promotion. vdW Theorem 3 is essentially a sequential-product reformulation.

    **Step 4: Exhibit the involution.**

    Given V = M_n(C)^sa, the C*-algebra is A = M_n(C), and the involution is:

    For X = a + ib where a, b in M_n(C)^sa (a is the "real part," b is the "imaginary part"):
    X* = a - ib = the conjugate transpose (Hermitian adjoint).

    Verify the involution properties:
    (a) *^2 = id: (a - ib)* = a + ib = X. Check.
    (b) (XY)* = Y*X*: Standard matrix algebra. Verify for 2x2 example.
    (c) A^sa = {X : X* = X}: X* = X iff b = 0, i.e., X = a in M_n(C)^sa. Check.
    (d) C*-identity: ||X*X|| = ||X||^2 where || || is operator norm. For M_n(C), this is the standard result.

    For the qubit case (n=2): exhibit the involution on M_2(C) concretely:
    [[alpha, beta], [gamma, delta]]* = [[conj(alpha), conj(gamma)], [conj(beta), conj(delta)]]
