    **Theorem 1 (No Spin(9)-equivariant complex structure):**
    Let V_{1/2} = S_9 be the 16-dimensional real spinor representation of Spin(9) arising from the Peirce decomposition of h_3(O) under E_{11}. There exists no Spin(9)-equivariant linear map J: V_{1/2} -> V_{1/2} with J^2 = -Id.

    Proof strategy:
    - S_9 is the unique irreducible real representation of Cl+(9,0) = M_16(R) (Lawson-Michelsohn, Table I.4.3).
    - By Schur's lemma, End_{Spin(9)}(S_9) = R (the division algebra associated to a real-type irrep).
    - The real-type classification follows from Bott periodicity: dimension 9 mod 8 = 1, and the Frobenius-Schur indicator is +1.
    - A Spin(9)-equivariant J with J^2 = -Id would be an element of End_{Spin(9)}(S_9) satisfying J^2 = -Id.
    - But End_{Spin(9)}(S_9) = R, and no real number satisfies x^2 = -1.
    - Therefore no such J exists. QED.

    Emphasize: this does NOT say "no complex structure exists on R^16." R^16 has many complex structures. The theorem says none of them commutes with the Spin(9) action.

    **Theorem 2 (J_u not in the Peirce Lie algebra):**
    The Krasnov complex structure J_u (left multiplication by u = e_7 on O^2) does not lie in the Lie algebra spin(9) generated by Peirce commutators [T_a, T_b] for a, b in V_0 = h_2(O).

    Proof strategy:
    - The Lie algebra spin(9) = span{[T_a, T_b] : a, b in V_0} consists entirely of grade-2 Clifford elements (commutators of grade-1 generators).
    - J_u has nonzero grade-3 Clifford components: ||J_u^{(3)}|| = 0.866, ||J_u^{(2)}|| = 0.500 (Phase 29-01, Eq. 29-01.3).
    - A grade-2-only subspace cannot contain an element with nonzero grade-3 components.
    - Therefore J_u is not in spin(9). QED.

    Note the subtlety: spin(9) DOES contain elements squaring to -Id (every gamma_{ij} for i != j satisfies gamma_{ij}^2 = -Id). The point is that NONE of these is J_u. J_u is a specific complex structure requiring grade-3 components.

    Also note: the hierarchy of Peirce-generated structures is:
    - Level 0: R*Id (from V_1 = R*E_{11}) -- 1-dim, no J possible
    - Level 1: span{T_b} -- 10-dim, all symmetric, no antisymmetric J possible (ALGV-02)
    - Level 2: spin(9) = span{[T_a,T_b]} -- 36-dim, grade-2 only, J_u not in it (this theorem)
    - Level 3: M_16(R) = associative closure -- 256-dim, contains everything (vacuous)

    **Theorem 3 (Weakest sufficient condition):**
    The minimal additional datum beyond the Peirce structure of h_3(O) under E_{11} that determines a complex structure on V_{1/2} with the Standard Model stabilizer is the choice of a unit imaginary octonion u in S^6 subset Im(O).

    Given such a u:
    (a) J_u: V_{1/2} -> V_{1/2} defined by left multiplication by u on O^2 satisfies J_u^2 = -Id.
    (b) J_u is the unique (up to sign) element of its 8-monomial Clifford subspace satisfying X^2 = -Id (Phase 29-02: Jacobian rank 8, tangent dim 0).
    (c) Stab_{spin(9)}(J_u) = su(3) + u(1)^2, dim = 10, containing the su(3) color factor of G_SM.
    (d) Any two choices u, u' in S^6 are related by a G_2 automorphism of O, so J_u and J_{u'} are G_2-conjugate (hence Spin(9)-conjugate since G_2 subset Spin(7) subset Spin(9)).

    This identifies the choice of u as precisely Gap B2 in Paper 7's 9-link chain.

    Note the Krasnov discrepancy: we find dim(stab) = 10 = su(3)+u(1)^2; Krasnov claims dim = 12 = su(3)+su(2)+u(1). The su(3) factor matches. The 2-dimensional discrepancy likely reflects different Spin(9) embeddings (Peirce-derived vs Krasnov's Cl(9)-based). State this explicitly.
