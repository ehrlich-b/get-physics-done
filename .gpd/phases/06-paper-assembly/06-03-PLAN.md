    **Section 7: Discussion (~2.5 pages)**

    7.1 Summary of Results: One paragraph restating the main theorem and the logical chain.

    7.2 Standing Assumptions: Detailed analysis of each:

    - Assumption 1 (finite-dimensional spectral OUS): "This is the most restrictive assumption. Infinite-dimensional generalizations would require type III von Neumann algebra theory. The spectral decomposition is essential for our product construction, and infinite-dimensional spectral theory introduces domain issues. We expect the core argument to extend to type I factors but leave this to future work."

    - Assumption 2 (faithful self-model): "A system that only approximately self-models (phi is injective but not surjective, or approximately isometric) would yield an approximate sequential product. The positivity bound would become an inequality rather than an equality, potentially selecting a non-maximal mixing function. This could yield a theory intermediate between classical and quantum. Exploring this is a natural direction for future work."

    - Assumption 3 (minimal composite): "The minimal composite construction is standard in the GPT literature. For complex QM, minimal = maximal (no entangled states beyond what product structure generates). This is a feature, not a bug: it means our assumption is exactly right for the theory we derive. However, for a priori exploration, one might ask whether maximal composites could yield different conclusions."

    - Assumption 4 (simple EJA): "The trace form non-degeneracy requires the EJA to be simple (no direct sum decomposition). Direct sums would decompose into simple summands, each of which would satisfy our theorem independently. So this assumption restricts to irreducible systems."

    7.3 Comparison with Other Programs: Discuss 3-4 programs in more depth than the table:
    - Masanes-Mueller (2013): closest in parsimony (3 axioms). Key difference: they assume tomographic locality, we derive it. Their continuous reversibility axiom has no analogue in our framework.
    - CDP (2011): purification axiom is their workhorse. We derive C*-structure without purification.
    - van de Wetering effectus theory (2019): same author provides our key theorems, but the effectus approach uses different axioms (~5) on categorical structure. Our approach is more operational (self-modeling) and uses fewer axioms.
    - Note on Alfsen-Shultz geometry: their characterization of C*-algebras via orientation on state spaces is geometrically beautiful but requires orientation as input. We derive the orientation from self-modeling.

    7.4 Future Directions:
    - Infinite-dimensional extension (most important)
    - Non-Markovian self-models (connection to quantum memory)
    - Approximate self-models (decoherence as failure of faithful self-modeling)
    - Connection to Barandes' stochastic-quantum bijection (mention briefly, cite)
    - Categorical formulation (connection to effectus theory)

    7.5 Conclusion: Two-paragraph conclusion. "We have shown that a single operational premise -- the existence of a faithful self-model -- is sufficient to derive the full structure of complex quantum mechanics for finite-dimensional systems. The complex field, the Jordan product, the C*-involution, and local tomography are all consequences of self-modeling, not independent postulates."

    A.1 Proof of S4 (Orthogonality Symmetry): Full proof from derivations/04-axiom-S4.md, adapted to paper notation (a . b instead of &). Include: theorem statement, Case A (full-rank), Case B (rank-deficient with facial absorption), phi-independence corollary.

    A.2 Proof of Local Tomography: Full proof from derivations/05-local-tomography.md. Include: correlation bilinear form definition, non-degeneracy argument, dimension counting, entangled-sector elimination via minimality.
