  <files>derivations/11-peirce-complexification.md</files>
  <action>
    Derive the Peirce decomposition of h_3(O) under the observer's rank-1 idempotent e = E_11 (the 3x3 matrix with 1 in the (1,1) entry, 0 elsewhere). This represents the observer choosing a "slot" in the Jordan algebra.

    Step 1: Write h_3(O) explicitly. Elements are 3x3 Hermitian matrices over O:
      X = [[alpha, x_3bar, x_2],
           [x_3, beta, x_1bar],
           [x_2bar, x_1, gamma]]
    where alpha, beta, gamma in R and x_1, x_2, x_3 in O, bar = octonion conjugation.
    dim(h_3(O)) = 3 + 3*8 = 27.

    Step 2: Compute the Peirce decomposition under e = E_11.
    The Jordan product is a * b = (1/2)(ab + ba) where multiplication is the usual matrix product with octonion entries.
    Peirce eigenspaces of L_e (where L_e(x) = e * x):
      - V_0 = {x : e * x = 0} -- elements with alpha = 0, x_2 = x_3 = 0 (the "1-complement")
      - V_{1/2} = {x : e * x = (1/2)x} -- off-diagonal elements involving row/column 1
      - V_1 = {x : e * x = x} -- the idempotent's own space, just multiples of e

    Identify explicitly:
      - V_1 = span{E_11} = R, dim = 1
      - V_{1/2} = {X with only x_2, x_3 nonzero} = O + O = O^2, dim = 16
      - V_0 = {X with alpha = 0, x_2 = x_3 = 0, beta, gamma in R, x_1 in O} = h_2(O), dim = 10

    Verify: 1 + 16 + 10 = 27.

    Step 3: Identify the stabilizer of e in F_4 = Aut(h_3(O)).
    The stabilizer Stab_{F_4}(e) = Spin(9). This acts on V_{1/2} = O^2 as the real 16-dim spinor representation S_9. Cite Baez "The Octonions" Sec 4.3 and Yokota.

    Key facts to state (with references):
    - F_4 = Aut(h_3(O)) has dim 52
    - Spin(9) has dim 36
    - S_9 is the unique real irreducible 16-dim representation of Spin(9)
    - The action of Spin(9) on V_{1/2} = O^2 is via this spinor representation
    - V_0 = h_2(O) carries the vector representation of Spin(9) (the 9-dim defining rep embeds in the 10-dim space via traceless part)

    Note: The Peirce decomposition here uses the idempotent e = E_11, which corresponds to the observer selecting one of the three diagonal slots. This is the "rank-1 idempotent choice" that appears as Gap B step 1 in the roadmap -- we take it as given input, not derived.
  </action>

    2. Peirce eigenvalue check: verify e * E_11 = E_11 (eigenvalue 1), e * (off-diagonal with x_2 or x_3) gives 1/2 of itself, e * (lower-right block) = 0

  <name>Task 2: C*-observer forces complexification and Spin(9) -> Spin(10) upgrade</name>
  <files>derivations/11-peirce-complexification.md</files>
  <action>
    Prove that the observer's C*-algebra nature forces complexification of V_{1/2} = O^2 and derive the Spin(9) -> Spin(10) upgrade. This is the central new argument of Phase 18.

    Step 4: State the Paper 5 result precisely.
    Paper 5 (v2.0) proved: a system that faithfully self-models must be described by M_n(C)^sa -- the self-adjoint part of a complex matrix algebra. The complex structure is NOT a choice; it is forced by local tomography (type exclusion, Phase 5 Plan 02). The observer is therefore a C*-algebra system: its state space is the self-adjoint part of a C*-algebra over C.

    Step 5: The complexification argument.
    The observer (a C*-algebra system, hence "complex") probes V_{1/2} = O^2 (a real vector space). The key insight:

    (a) The observer's measurement outcomes are elements of C (the observer's scalar field), not R.
    (b) When the observer constructs expectation values, linear functionals, or any algebraic operations on V_{1/2}, these operations are C-linear (because the observer's algebraic structure is over C).
    (c) Therefore, the observer's effective description of V_{1/2} is:
        V_{1/2}^C := V_{1/2} tensor_R C
    This is the extension of scalars from R to C.

    More precisely: the observer acts on h_3(O) through its own C*-algebra structure. The Peirce projection P_{1/2} that extracts V_{1/2} is a linear map. When composed with the observer's C-linear state space, the resulting "observed Peirce space" is V_{1/2} tensor_R C.

    This is NOT assuming complexification -- it is deriving it from the fact that a C*-algebra observer necessarily works with complex-linear structures. The observer cannot "see" V_{1/2} as a real space; it sees V_{1/2}^C because its own algebraic operations are complex.

    Step 6: Compute V_{1/2}^C.
    V_{1/2} = O^2 has dim_R = 16.
    V_{1/2}^C = O^2 tensor_R C has dim_C = 16 (the real dimension stays 32, but as a complex vector space it is 16-dimensional).

    Step 7: Spin(9) -> Spin(10) upgrade.
    Before complexification: Spin(9) acts on V_{1/2} = O^2 = S_9 (real 16-dim spinor).

    After complexification: the Spin(9) action extends C-linearly to V_{1/2}^C. Now use the standard representation theory fact:

    - Spin(9) subset Spin(10) as the stabilizer of a vector in the 10-dim defining representation.
    - S_9 (real 16) complexifies to S_9^C = S_10^+ (complex 16-dim Weyl spinor of Spin(10)).

    This is because:
    - Spin(10) has two Weyl spinors S_10^+ and S_10^-, each of complex dimension 16.
    - Under restriction to Spin(9): S_10^+ |_{Spin(9)} = S_9^C, where S_9 is the unique real 16-dim Spin(9) spinor.
    - Equivalently: the real spinor S_9 is the "real form" of S_10^+.

    State this chain:
    V_{1/2} = S_9 (real, dim_R = 16) --[complexification]--> V_{1/2}^C = S_9 tensor_R C = S_10^+ (complex, dim_C = 16)

    The symmetry upgrade is: Stab_{F_4}(e) = Spin(9) upgrades to Spin(10) because the complexified spinor admits the larger symmetry group.

    Cross-check with Boyle 2020: Boyle identifies exactly this structure in h_3^C(O), where the Peirce V_{1/2} of the complexified Jordan algebra carries the Spin(10) Weyl spinor. Our argument derives the complexification rather than starting from h_3^C(O).

    Step 8: Note what this does NOT yet establish.
    - The full F_4 -> E_6 upgrade (Plan 02)
    - The Peirce decomposition of h_3^C(O) = h_3(O) tensor_R C (Plan 02)
    - The 27 -> 1 + 10 + 16 decomposition under Spin(10) (Plan 02)

    If the complexification argument seems to require more than just C*-nature (e.g., a specific embedding of the observer into h_3(O), or additional data), document this honestly as a backtracking trigger finding and assess whether the additional input is derivable from self-modeling.
  </action>

    4. Branching rule check: S_10^+|_{Spin(9)} = S_9 tensor_R C, consistent with Spin(9) subset Spin(10)
