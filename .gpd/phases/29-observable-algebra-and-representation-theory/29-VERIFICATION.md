## Krasnov Discrepancy: Fully Resolved

The dim=10 (our result) vs dim=12 (Krasnov) discrepancy was the most important open question. The verifier resolved it computationally:

1. **Built Krasnov's exact Cl(9) representation** from his Eq. (52): gamma^i = ((0,E^i),(-E^i,0)), gamma^8 = ((0,I),(I,0)), gamma^9 = ((I,0),(0,-I)).
2. **Built Krasnov's J_R** = diag(R_{e4}, R_{e4}) (right multiplication by e^4, his Eq. 35).
3. **Computed stabilizer of J_R in Krasnov's spin(9):** dim = 12. Matches Krasnov's Eq. (62).
4. **Verified the two spin(9) subalgebras are DIFFERENT:** combined rank of Peirce spin(9) and Krasnov spin(9) = 51 (not 36). They are genuinely different 36-dim subalgebras of M_16(R).

**Root cause:** The Peirce algebra T_b operators come from the Jordan product structure of h_3(O), which produces a DIFFERENT Cl(9) representation than Krasnov's direct construction from left-multiplication matrices. Both representations are irreducible Cl(9,0) on R^16, but they are not identical (they are related by an orthogonal transformation that is NOT the identity). The stabilizer of J_u depends on WHICH spin(9) is used.

**Impact on Phase 30:** This is a significant finding for Gap C. The Peirce-derived spin(9) (physically motivated by h_3(O) and the observer algebra) gives a SMALLER stabilizer (dim=10 = su(3)+u(1)^2) than Krasnov's spin(9) (dim=12 = su(2)+u(1)+su(3)). The missing 2 dimensions correspond to the su(2) weak factor being reduced to u(1)^2 in the Peirce representation. Phase 30 must determine whether this is physically significant or whether it reflects a mismatch in how the Peirce structure embeds Spin(9).
