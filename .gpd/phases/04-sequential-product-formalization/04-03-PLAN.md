    For each axiom, quote the exact statement from arXiv:1803.11139 Definition 2, then prove it holds for the self-modeling sequential product defined in Plan 01.

    **S1 (Additivity in second argument):**
    Statement: a & (b + c) = a & b + a & c when b + c <= 1.
    Proof strategy: The product a & b is defined via compressions C_a (or spectral extension thereof). Compressions are LINEAR maps, so C_a(b + c) = C_a(b) + C_a(c). For the spectral extension a = sum lambda_i p_i, we have a & (b+c) = sum lambda_i C_{p_i}(b+c) = sum lambda_i [C_{p_i}(b) + C_{p_i}(c)] = a & b + a & c.
    Must reference: linearity of compressions (Alfsen-Shultz); spectral extension from Plan 01.

    **S2 (Continuity in first argument):**
    Statement: a -> a & b is continuous in the order unit norm.
    Proof strategy: In finite dimensions, a & b = sum lambda_i C_{p_i}(b) where a = sum lambda_i p_i. The map a -> a & b is a composition of the spectral decomposition (continuous in finite dim) and linear maps (continuous). Alternatively: all linear maps on finite-dimensional normed spaces are continuous, and the spectral extension is a finite linear combination.
    Must reference: finite-dimensionality; vdW Corollary 7 (spectral decomposition).

    **S3 (Unitality):**
    Statement: 1 & a = a for all effects a.
    Proof strategy: The unit 1 is a sharp effect (1 & 1 = 1, 1 & 0 = 0). The compression C_1 is the identity map (compression for the maximal face = entire space). Therefore 1 & a = C_1(a) = a.
    Must reference: Plan 01 definition; the fact that C_1 = id follows from Alfsen-Shultz (compression for maximal face).

    **S5 (Associativity of compatible effects):**
    Statement: If a | b (i.e., a & b = b & a), then a & (b & c) = (a & b) & c.
    Proof strategy: When a | b, the compressions C_a and C_b commute (Niestegge Lemma 3.3, adapted to the phi-parametrized setting). Commutativity of compressions C_a C_b = C_b C_a implies:
    a & (b & c) = C_a(C_b(c)) = (C_a C_b)(c) = (C_b C_a)(c) = ... needs careful handling of the spectral extension.
    For sharp compatible effects: (C_p C_q)(c) = C_{p^q}(c) where p ^ q = p & q (Prop. 12 of vdW). Then (p & q) & c = C_{p&q}(c) = C_{p^q}(c) and p & (q & c) = C_p(C_q(c)). These are equal when C_p C_q = C_{p^q} (the compression for the meet).
    Must verify: this algebraic identity extends to general (non-sharp) compatible effects via spectral decomposition.
    Must reference: Niestegge Lemma 3.3; vdW Prop. 12.

    **S6 (Additivity of compatible effects):**
    Statement: If a | b then a | (1-b), and if also a | c then a | (b+c) when b+c <= 1.
    Proof strategy: Compatibility means a & b = b & a.
    (i) a | (1-b): a & (1-b) = a & 1 - a & b = a - a & b (by S1). And (1-b) & a = 1 & a - b & a = a - b & a (by S1). Since a & b = b & a, we get a & (1-b) = (1-b) & a.
    (ii) a | (b+c): a & (b+c) = a & b + a & c (by S1). And (b+c) & a = b & a + c & a (by S1, noting b+c <= 1). Since a | b and a | c: a & b = b & a and a & c = c & a. Therefore a & (b+c) = (b+c) & a.
    Must reference: S1 (proved above); definition of compatibility.

    **S7 (Multiplicativity of compatible effects):**
    Statement: If a | b and a | c then a | (b & c).
    Proof strategy: Must show a & (b & c) = (b & c) & a. Using compressions: a & (b & c) = C_a(C_b(c)). And (b & c) & a = C_{b&c}(a). When a | b and a | c, the compressions C_a commutes with C_b (by compatibility) and with C_c. Then C_a(C_b(c)) = C_b(C_a(c)) (commutativity with C_b), and we need to connect this to C_{b&c}(a).
    This is the most subtle of the "easy" axioms. For sharp effects: if p | q and p | r, then C_p commutes with C_q and C_r, hence with C_{q&r} (since q & r = C_q(r) by Plan 01). Then p & (q & r) = C_p(C_q(r)) and (q & r) & p = C_{q&r}(p). The equality follows from C_p C_q = C_q C_p applied twice.
    Extension to general effects via spectral decomposition.
    Must reference: Niestegge Lemma 3.3; compression algebra structure.
