  <name>Task 1: Construct and verify non-associativity witness</name>
  <files>derivations/04-non-associativity.md</files>
  <action>
    Exhibit an explicit triple (a, b, c) of effects in a finite-dimensional OUS such that (a & b) & c != a & (b & c) for the self-modeling sequential product defined in Plan 01.

    **Step 1 -- Choose the test space.**
    Work in M_2(C)^sa (the 4-dimensional real vector space of 2x2 Hermitian matrices). This is the simplest non-classical case. Use the self-modeling sequential product from Plan 01 with phi = identity (maximal model, M = B).

    **Step 2 -- Choose candidate effects.**
    Select three effects a, b, c that are NOT mutually compatible (i.e., at least two do not commute under the sequential product). Natural choice: effects corresponding to measurements along different Bloch sphere axes.

    For example:
    - a = (1/2)(I + (1/2) sigma_z) = [[3/4, 0], [0, 1/4]]
    - b = (1/2)(I + (1/2) sigma_x) = [[1/2, 1/4], [1/4, 1/2]]
    - c = (1/2)(I + (1/2) sigma_y) = [[1/2, -i/4], [i/4, 1/2]]

    **Step 3 -- Compute both sides exactly.**
    Using the self-modeling product definition from Plan 01:
    - Compute a & b (using compressions and spectral extension)
    - Compute (a & b) & c
    - Compute b & c
    - Compute a & (b & c)
    - Compute the difference Delta = (a & b) & c - a & (b & c)

    All arithmetic must be exact (rational or algebraic numbers, no floating-point).

    **Step 4 -- Verify nonzero.**
    Show Delta is nonzero by exhibiting at least one nonzero matrix entry.

    **Step 5 -- Cross-check with Luders product.**
    For comparison, verify that the standard Luders product a & b = sqrt(a) b sqrt(a) is also non-associative for the same triple. This is a consistency check (both products should be non-associative, though the specific Delta values will differ).

    **Step 6 -- State the implication.**
    By Westerbaan-Westerbaan-vdW (2020): since the self-modeling sequential product is non-associative, the algebra is NOT commutative. This is necessary (not sufficient) for the algebra to be quantum-mechanical. CITE the result; do NOT re-derive it.

    **STOP CONDITION:** If after testing 10+ triples (including randomly generated effects), ALL give (a & b) & c = a & (b & c), then the product is likely associative. Escalate to the user: "Non-associativity check failed. The self-modeling sequential product appears associative, which would make the algebra commutative (classical). The sequential product route may be dead."
  </action>

    3. Delta has at least one nonzero entry (verified symbolically, not numerically)
    4. Cross-check: the Luders product is also non-associative for the same triple
