    The SSB pattern (from Plan 01) is Spin(9) -> Spin(8). The 36 generators of Spin(9) split as:
    - 28 Spin(8) generators (unbroken): G_{ab} = [T_a, T_b] for a,b in {0,...,7} (indices within the first 8 of 9)
    - 8 broken generators: G_{a,8} = [T_a, T_8] for a in {0,...,7} (the coset so(9)/so(8))

    Here T_0,...,T_8 are the 9 Clifford generators already computed in Phase 38. The ordered ground state selects the direction T_8 (by convention -- the specific direction doesn't matter by SO(9) symmetry).

    Step 1: Identify the 8 broken generators Q_a = G_{a,8} = [T_a, T_8] for a = 0,...,7. These are 16x16 matrices already available from the Spin(9) generator construction in code/effective_hamiltonian.py.

    Step 2: Choose a specific ground state. The 2-site ground state is in Lambda^1(V_9), dim 9. For the single-site order parameter: in the thermodynamic limit, the symmetry-broken state has <T_8> = m (magnetization along direction 8). The ground state |GS> is the eigenstate of T_8 with maximum eigenvalue.

    IMPORTANT: T_8 is a 16x16 matrix with eigenvalues +/- 1/2 (from Clifford algebra {T_a,T_b} = (1/2) delta I_16, so T_8^2 = (1/4) I_16, eigenvalues = +/- 1/2). The ground state |GS> is the eigenspace of T_8 with eigenvalue +1/2 (dim 8, since 16/2 = 8).

    Step 3: Compute rho_ab = <GS| [Q_a, Q_b] |GS> for a,b in {0,...,7}.

    Since |GS> is the +1/2 eigenspace of T_8 (dim 8), and the ground state in the thermodynamic limit selects a specific vector within this eigenspace (or an equal-weight sum), we need:
    - For a SINGLE ordered state: pick any normalized vector |psi> in the T_8 = +1/2 eigenspace
    - Compute rho_ab = <psi| [Q_a, Q_b] |psi>
    - But rho_ab is state-dependent within the eigenspace. The RELEVANT rho_ab for Watanabe-Murayama is the EXTENSIVE quantity in the thermodynamic limit: rho_ab = (1/V) <GS_N | [Q_a^total, Q_b^total] |GS_N> where Q_a^total = sum_i Q_a^(i).

    KEY SUBTLETY: For the N-site ferromagnetic ground state, all sites are aligned. The charge density operator q_a = Q_a (on-site) has expectation <q_a q_b - q_b q_a> in the single-site ground state. The extensive rho_ab = N * <single site commutator>, and the intensive rho_ab (per site) = <psi_0 | [Q_a, Q_b] | psi_0> where |psi_0> is the single-site ordered state.

    CAREFUL: [Q_a, Q_b] = [G_{a,8}, G_{b,8}] = [[T_a,T_8], [T_b,T_8]]. This is a commutator of commutators. Compute it from the Clifford algebra.

    Compute using Clifford algebra identities:
    [T_a, T_8] = 2 T_a T_8 - {T_a, T_8} = 2 T_a T_8 - (1/2) delta_{a,8} I = 2 T_a T_8 (since a != 8 for broken generators)
    So Q_a = 2 T_a T_8 for a = 0,...,7.
    [Q_a, Q_b] = [2 T_a T_8, 2 T_b T_8] = 4 (T_a T_8 T_b T_8 - T_b T_8 T_a T_8)

    Implement in code/effective_hamiltonian.py:
    - broken_generators_spin9_to_spin8(): returns list of 8 matrices Q_a
    - rho_ab_matrix(ground_state): computes the 8x8 antisymmetric matrix rho_ab
    - Choose |psi_0> as a specific eigenvector of T_8 with eigenvalue +1/2
    - Check that rho_ab is antisymmetric
    - Compute rank(rho_ab) and eigenvalues

    Add tests verifying:
    - Q_a are 16x16 antisymmetric matrices (generators of so(9))
    - rho_ab is 8x8 antisymmetric
    - rank(rho_ab) is even (symplectic constraint)
  </action>
