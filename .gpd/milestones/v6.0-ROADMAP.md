## Milestone Summary

**Key Results:**
- V_1 = R*E_11 is 1-dimensional; Peirce action L_{E_11}: V_{1/2} -> V_{1/2} is scalar multiplication by 1/2
- No C*-subalgebra exists inside h_3(O) (Shirshov-Cohn theorem) -- observer is necessarily external
- P_1(v*w) = [Re(x3 bar(y3)) + Re(x2 bar(y2))] E_11 is a real scalar -- state-effect pairing yields only R^16 inner product
- h_3(O) GNS gives R^27 (real inner product space), not a complex Hilbert space
- A tensor_R V = A tensor_C V^C is a generic algebraic tautology for any real V and any C-algebra A
- Compatible complex structures on R^16 form O(16)/U(8) (56-dimensional), none selected by the Peirce interface

**Key Decisions:**
- [Phase 22, Plan 01]: Route 1 NEGATIVE -- conditional expectations do not force complexification
- [Phase 22, Plan 02]: Route 2 NEGATIVE -- state-effect duality is intrinsically real through Peirce
- [Phase 22, Plan 03]: Route 3 NEGATIVE -- three independent obstructions
- [Phase 22, Plan 04]: Route 4 WEAK POSITIVE -- generic tautology, equivalent to Phase 18 extension-of-scalars
- Phases 23-25 cancelled due to lack of positive Phase 22 result

**Issues Deferred:**
- Gap C remains open in Paper 7
- The complexification step in the L1-L9 chain needs either: (a) acceptance as a framework-level argument (extension of scalars), or (b) a different approach entirely

**Lessons Learned:**
- The V_1 = R bottleneck is structural: for any rank-1 idempotent e in h_3(O), the Peirce 1-space V_1(e) = R*e is 1-dimensional
- "Observer probes V_{1/2}" is too vague -- the specific algebraic interface (Peirce multiplication) strips out all complex structure before it reaches V_{1/2}
- Extension of scalars (A tensor_R V = A tensor_C V^C) is always available but proves nothing specific about h_3(O)
