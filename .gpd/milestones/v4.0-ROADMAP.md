# Milestone: v4.0 Spectral Triple from Self-Modeling

**Status:** Closed 2026-03-23 (medium success -- SM not achieved)
**Phases:** 13-15 completed, 16-17 abandoned
**Total Plans:** 7 (3 + 2 + 2)

## Overview

Investigated whether the doubled self-modeling composite (H, J, gamma) satisfies the axioms of a real spectral triple of KO-dimension 6 and whether the first-order condition forces the Standard Model algebra C + H + M_3(C). The order zero condition and D moduli space were successfully established, but the first-order condition revealed a structural obstruction: simple M_n(C) cannot produce the SM gauge group. Phases 16-17 (remaining axioms and paper assembly) were abandoned as the approach was superseded.

## Milestone Summary

**Key Results:**
- Order zero condition [pi(a), pi_o(b)] = 0 verified at general n (Phase 13)
- H = 2 x C^{n^2} as irreducible bimodules; per-sector n^2 = k^2, giving k=4 (SM value) at n=4
- D moduli space dim = n^2(n^2+1); Barrett-form D with real symmetric K = linearized sequential product (Jordan product)
- First-order condition: Barrett D gives A_F = M_n(C) (trivially satisfied); general D gives A_F = C
- **Structural obstruction: simple M_n(C) cannot produce C + H + M_3(C). SM requires direct sum algebra.**

**Key Equations:**
- pi_o(b) = diag(I_n tensor b^T, I_n tensor b^T)
- D moduli: dim = n^2(n^2+1) (272 at n=4)
- Barrett D: D_K(X) = KX + XK = 2(K * X) (Jordan product)
- Master formula: [[M, L_a], R_b](X) = sum_k [A_k, a] X [b, B_k]

**Key Decisions:**
- Naive algebra action pi(a) = diag(a tensor I, a tensor I) used as primary
- Barrett-form D identified as self-modeling Dirac operator (Jordan product connection)
- Medium success tier acknowledged: valid spectral triple with U(n), not SM

**Lessons Learned:**
- Simple algebra starting point is structurally incompatible with SM gauge group
- The CCM classification requires a direct sum algebra (e.g., M_2(H) + M_4(C)) as input
- Barrett-form D has a beautiful connection to self-modeling (Jordan product = linearized sequential product) but the algebra is too simple
- The first-order condition either imposes no restriction (Barrett) or kills everything except scalars (general D) -- no middle ground for simple algebras

**Open Questions:**
- Can a direct sum algebra arise naturally from self-modeling (e.g., from multiple subsystem types)?
- Does the Boyle-Farnsworth Jordan geometry framework offer an alternative route?
- Is there a way to break the algebra simplicity while preserving self-modeling motivation?
