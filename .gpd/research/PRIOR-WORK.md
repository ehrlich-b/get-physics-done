## Foundational Work

### Alfsen-Hanche-Olsen-Shultz (1980) - State Space Characterization of C*-algebras

**Key contribution:** Proved that a JB-algebra A is (isomorphic to) the self-adjoint part of a C*-algebra if and only if its state space satisfies two conditions: (1) the 3-ball property (every face generated by two pure states is a line segment or a 3-ball), and (2) orientability (a consistent global choice of orientation on all 3-ball faces).

**Method:** The 3-ball property guarantees a faithful Jordan representation on a complex Hilbert space. Orientability guarantees the representation can be chosen so that the image is the self-adjoint part of the C*-algebra it generates.

**Critical implication for v8.0:** This is the precise theorem connecting Jordan structure to complex structure. A JB-algebra "acquires" C*-structure (and hence complex structure) if and only if its state space is orientable. The converse is equally important: a JB-algebra whose state space is NOT orientable CANNOT be the self-adjoint part of any C*-algebra. For h_3(O), which is exceptional and whose state space fails the 3-ball property, this means h_3(O) itself cannot be a C*-algebra. But the question for v8.0 is different: it is whether the OBSERVER's C*-structure (which exists by Paper 5) propagates to V_{1/2} through Peirce multiplication.

**Limitations:** The theorem characterizes WHICH Jordan algebras are C*-algebras. It does not directly address the question of whether a C*-algebra acting on a non-C* Jordan module forces the module to acquire complex structure. The module question is logically distinct from the algebra characterization question.

**Relevance:** Establishes the theoretical framework. The observer IS M_n(C)^sa (a JB-algebra satisfying both conditions). The universe h_3(O) is a JB-algebra that fails the 3-ball property (exceptional). The question is whether the observer's orientability can propagate through Peirce interface maps.

**Reference:** Alfsen-Hanche-Olsen-Shultz, "State spaces of C*-algebras," Acta Math. 144 (1980), 267-305.

### Connes (1974) / Alfsen-Shultz (2001) - Orientation and Dynamics

**Key contribution:** Connes first realized that orientation determines the associative (non-Jordan) part of the C*-product. Given a JBW-algebra A (Jordan algebra of self-adjoint operators), a Connes orientation is an assignment I that provides the "i" needed to go from the Jordan product a o b = (ab+ba)/2 to the full associative product. Precisely: I(delta_d) = delta_{id} for each d in A + iA, where delta_d(a) = d o a is the Jordan derivation.

**Critical implication for v8.0:** This theorem says that the complex structure "i" is NOT part of the Jordan algebra itself -- it is ADDITIONAL structure (the orientation) that must be supplied. For the observer M_n(C)^sa, this orientation exists and is canonical (it is the standard i of C). The question is whether this orientation, which lives on the observer's algebra, can be "transmitted" to V_{1/2} through the Peirce multiplication maps. The Connes-Alfsen-Shultz framework says orientation determines dynamics, so the observer's orientation should determine the observer's dynamics on V_{1/2}.

**Limitations:** The theorem applies to algebras, not to modules. Whether the orientation of an algebra acting on a module induces an orientation on the module is not addressed.

**Relevance:** Provides the mathematical language for what "complex structure propagation" means: it is propagation of a Connes orientation through Peirce multiplication.

**References:**
- Connes, "Caracterisation des espaces vectoriels ordonnes sous-jacents aux algebres de von Neumann," Ann. Inst. Fourier 24 (1974), 121-155.
- Alfsen-Shultz, "State Spaces of Operator Algebras," Birkhauser (2001), Ch. 5-9.
- Alfsen-Shultz, "Geometry of State Spaces of Operator Algebras," Birkhauser (2003), Ch. 8-11.

### Krasnov (2019) - SO(9) Characterization of the SM Gauge Group

**Key contribution:** Provided a direct geometric characterization: G_SM is the subgroup of Spin(9) that commutes with a specific complex structure J on the space O^2 of Spin(9) spinors. This complex structure J is determined by a choice of unit imaginary octonion u in S^6.

**Method:** Exploits the octonionic split O = C + C^3 induced by u. The complex structure J on O^2 = V_{1/2} is precisely left-multiplication by u on each octonionic factor. The subgroup of Spin(9) commuting with J is exactly G_SM = [SU(3) x SU(2) x U(1)] / Z_6.

**Critical implication for v8.0:** Krasnov's result reformulates the problem. Instead of asking "does the observer force complexification of V_{1/2}?" we can ask "does the observer force a complex structure J on O^2?" The complex structure J on O^2 is left-multiplication by u -- an operation defined by octonionic multiplication, not by Peirce multiplication. But the observer's u (which determines its C-structure via O = C_u + C_u^3) is exactly the data that defines J. So the question reduces to: does the observer's internal complex structure u propagate to define J on O^2?

**Key distinction from Boyle:** Boyle (2020) starts from the complexified algebra h_3(O)^C and studies its automorphism group E_6. Krasnov stays within the real algebra and characterizes G_SM as a stabilizer subgroup of Spin(9). Krasnov's approach is closer to what v8.0 needs: a real-algebraic characterization that uses a complex structure as additional data on O^2, not as complexification of the ambient algebra.

**Limitations:** Krasnov does not derive the complex structure J from any dynamical or self-modeling principle. He characterizes G_SM given J, but does not explain why J exists. This is exactly Gap C.

**Relevance:** DIRECTLY relevant. Krasnov's J on O^2 is the complex structure v8.0 needs to derive. If the observer's C*-nature forces J on V_{1/2} = O^2, then G_SM follows by Krasnov's theorem.

**Reference:** Krasnov, "SO(9) characterisation of the Standard Model gauge group," arXiv:1912.11282 (2019, revised 2020).

### Effros-Stormer (1979) - Positive Projections and Jordan Structure

**Key contribution:** Established that positive projections (conditional expectations) on C*-algebras produce Jordan subalgebras. The image of a contractive positive projection on a C*-algebra is a JC-algebra (Jordan subalgebra of a C*-algebra).

**Method:** If P: A -> A is a positive unital projection on a C*-algebra with P^2 = P and ||P|| = 1, then P(A) with the product a o_P b = P(a o b) is a JC-algebra.

**Relevance to v8.0:** The Peirce projection P_1: h_3(O) -> V_1 is a positive projection. Since V_1 = R * E_{11} is 1-dimensional, it is trivially a JC-algebra (isomorphic to R). The Peirce projection P_{1/2}: h_3(O) -> V_{1/2} is also a positive projection, but V_{1/2} = O^2 is NOT a Jordan subalgebra (the Peirce multiplication rules give V_{1/2} o V_{1/2} subset V_1 + V_0, not V_{1/2}). So V_{1/2} is a Jordan MODULE for V_1, not a Jordan algebra. The Effros-Stormer theorem applies to the projections but does not directly address the module question.

**Reference:** Effros-Stormer, "Positive projections and Jordan structure in operator algebras," Math. Scand. 45 (1979), 127-138.

### Hanche-Olsen (1983) - JC-Algebra Structure and Tensor Products

**Key contribution:** Proved structure theorems for JC-algebras (Jordan algebras that embed in C*-algebras). Key result: a JB-algebra is a JC-algebra if and only if it satisfies certain identities involving the squares of elements (no exceptional part). Also established that the exceptional part of any JB-algebra is an ideal isomorphic to a direct sum of copies of h_3(O).

**The Hanche-Olsen-Stormer decomposition:** Every JB-algebra A decomposes as A = A_sp + A_exc, where A_sp is a JC-algebra (special part) and A_exc is a direct sum of copies of h_3(O) (exceptional part). This is the Jordan-algebraic analog of the Wedderburn decomposition.

**Critical implication for v8.0:** The exceptional factor h_3(O) has no C*-envelope. It cannot be embedded in any C*-algebra. This means the observer's C*-algebra M_n(C)^sa is fundamentally "outside" h_3(O) -- it cannot be a Jordan subalgebra of h_3(O). The observer interacts with h_3(O) THROUGH the Peirce interface, not by being a subalgebra of it.

**Relevance:** Confirms the v6.0 finding that the observer is necessarily external. The question for v8.0 is: what structure does the external observer's C*-nature impose on the Peirce module V_{1/2} when it acts through Peirce multiplication?

**Reference:** Hanche-Olsen, "On the structure and tensor products of JC-algebras," Can. J. Math. 35 (1983), 1059-1074.

### Boyle (2020) - The Standard Model, the Exceptional Jordan Algebra, and Triality

**Key contribution:** Showed that complexifying h_3(O) to h_3(O)^C upgrades the structure group from F_4 to E_6, and the 27-dimensional representation decomposes as 27_C = 1 + 10 + 16 under E_6 -> Spin(10) x U(1), where the 16 is a Weyl spinor S_{10}^+. Connected to triality: three generations from SO(8) triality acting on the three off-diagonal octonionic entries.

**The branching rule (established, HIGH confidence):**
- S_{10}^+ |_{Spin(9)} = S_9^C (complexified real spinor, multiplicity-free)
- F_4 -> E_6 via complexification; E_6 is the structure group of h_3(O)^C

**Critical implication for v8.0:** Boyle ASSUMES complexification; he does not derive it. His Section 2 simply passes to h_3(O)^C. The v8.0 question is whether the observer's C*-nature provides the physical justification for this step. Boyle's representation theory is the downstream consequence IF complexification is established.

**Relevance:** Provides the mathematical framework for what happens AFTER complexification. If v8.0 closes Gap C, Boyle's results complete the chain to the SM gauge group.

**Reference:** Boyle, "The Standard Model, the Exceptional Jordan Algebra, and Triality," arXiv:2006.16265 (2020).

### Farnsworth (2025) - The n-point Exceptional Universe

**Key contribution:** Solved an open problem: constructing finite-dimensional discrete spectral geometries coordinatized by non-simple exceptional Jordan algebras. Built a 2-point geometry with F_4 x F_4 gauge group. Established a framework where distinct "points" couple through an action on 1-forms constructed as split Jordan bimodules.

**Relevance to v8.0:** This is the most recent work on exceptional Jordan algebra spectral triples. The "split Jordan bimodules" used to couple distinct points are structurally analogous to the Peirce V_{1/2} module that couples V_1 (observer) to V_0 (environment). Farnsworth's construction may provide the mathematical language needed to formalize how the observer's C*-nature acts on V_{1/2} as a bimodule.

**Limitations:** The paper focuses on n-point (multi-observer) geometries, not on the single-observer Peirce decomposition question. Does not directly address complexification from a C*-observer.

**Reference:** Farnsworth, "The n-point Exceptional Universe," arXiv:2503.10744 (2025).

### Besnard-Farnsworth (2022) - Jordan Spectral Triples

**Key contribution:** Defined spectral triples with Jordan coordinate algebras replacing associative C*-algebras. Key result: gauge fields are automatically unimodular in the Jordan framework (resolving a long-standing problem in NCG).

**Relevance to v8.0:** Provides the formal definition of what a "Jordan spectral triple" IS. If v8.0 shows that C*-observer maps force complexification of V_{1/2}, the resulting structure should fit into the Besnard-Farnsworth framework as a Jordan spectral triple on h_3(O) with complexified V_{1/2}.

**Reference:** Besnard-Farnsworth, "Particle models from special Jordan backgrounds and spectral triples," arXiv:2206.07039 (2022); published J. Math. Phys. 63 (2022) 103505.

## Open Questions

1. **Does the observer's Connes orientation propagate through Peirce multiplication to V_{1/2}?** -- The observer M_n(C)^sa has a canonical Connes orientation (the standard "i"). Peirce multiplication L_a: V_{1/2} -> V_{1/2} for a in V_1 is R-linear. The question is whether the observer's orientation forces L to extend to a C-linear map on some complexification of V_{1/2}. No theorem in the literature addresses this specific question. The Alfsen-Shultz theory addresses orientation of ALGEBRAS, not of MODULES under algebra actions.

2. **Is the complex structure J on O^2 (Krasnov's J) derivable from the observer's C*-nature?** -- Krasnov defines J by left-multiplication by u on each octonion factor. The observer's complex structure IS u (Paper 7, Gap B2). The question is whether the observer's use of u for its own C*-structure automatically defines J on V_{1/2} through the Peirce interface. This is a precise formulation of Gap C.

3. **Is there a counterexample: a C*-algebra acting on a real Jordan module where the action does NOT force complexification?** -- Consider M_2(C)^sa acting on R^3 (the spin-1 representation of su(2)). The action is R-linear. The representation does not force R^3 to acquire a complex structure. This is a COUNTEREXAMPLE to naive "C*-actions always complexify." But the counterexample may not apply to the h_3(O) setting because: (a) the action is through Peirce multiplication, not through a representation, and (b) the octonion structure provides additional algebraic constraints.

4. **Does the observable algebra of the observer on V_{1/2} distinguish the h_3(O) case from generic real modules?** -- The observer measures V_{1/2} through maps of the form L_a for a in V_1. For generic Jordan algebras, V_1 can be multi-dimensional. For h_3(O) with rank-1 idempotent, V_1 = R (1-dim). This extreme constraint means the Peirce measurement interface is maximally restricted. The question is whether the octonionic structure of V_{1/2} = O^2 compensates -- i.e., whether the non-associativity of O provides additional structure that a generic R-module would lack.

5. **Does the Farnsworth bimodule framework (2025) provide the right language for the V_{1/2} complexification question?** -- Farnsworth's split Jordan bimodules couple distinct "points" (observers). V_{1/2} IS the bimodule coupling V_1 to V_0. The split bimodule structure might provide the rigorous framework for stating and proving Gap C.

**Statement:** For h_3(O) with rank-1 idempotent e = E_{11}, the Peirce 1-space is V_1 = R * E_{11}, a 1-dimensional real vector space.

**Proven/Conjectured:** Proven (standard result)

**Reference:** Baez, "The Octonions," Bull. AMS 39 (2002), Sec. 3.4; Paper 7 Proposition 3.

**Relevance:** This is the obstruction that killed all 4 routes in v6.0. With V_1 = R, the Peirce multiplication L_a for a in V_1 acts as scalar multiplication on V_{1/2}. ### Result 2: Alfsen-Shultz-Hanche-Olsen State Space Characterization

**Statement:** A JB-algebra A is isomorphic to the self-adjoint part of a C*-algebra if and only if the state space K of A has the 3-ball property and is orientable.

**Proven/Conjectured:** Proven

**Reference:** Alfsen-Hanche-Olsen-Shultz, Acta Math. 144 (1980), 267-305; Alfsen-Shultz, "State Spaces of Operator Algebras" (2001), Theorem 9.35.

**Relevance:** Establishes the precise conditions for a Jordan algebra to be "complex" (i.e., the self-adjoint part of a C*-algebra). The observer M_n(C)^sa satisfies these conditions. h_3(O) does NOT (exceptional: fails 3-ball property). The question is whether the observer's orientation can be partially transmitted to V_{1/2} through Peirce multiplication, even though h_3(O) as a whole cannot be C*.

### Result 3: Krasnov's Complex Structure Characterization of G_SM

**Statement:** G_SM = {g in Spin(9) : g J = J g} where J is left-multiplication by a unit imaginary octonion u on the space O^2 of Spin(9) spinors.

**Proven/Conjectured:** Proven

**Reference:** Krasnov, arXiv:1912.11282 (2019/2020).

**Relevance:** Reformulates Gap C as: does the observer's complex structure u define the complex structure J on V_{1/2} = O^2? If yes, then G_SM follows. Krasnov assumes J exists; v8.0 must derive J from the observer's C*-nature.

### Result 4: Peirce Multiplication Rules for h_3(O)

**Statement:** The Peirce spaces satisfy: V_1 o V_1 subset V_1; V_{1/2} o V_{1/2} subset V_1 + V_0; V_0 o V_0 subset V_0; V_1 o V_{1/2} subset V_{1/2}; V_0 o V_{1/2} subset V_{1/2}; V_1 o V_0 = {0}.

**Proven/Conjectured:** Proven (general Jordan algebra theory)

**Reference:** McCrimmon, "A Taste of Jordan Algebras" (2004), Theorem 5.4.5; Faraut-Koranyi, "Analysis on Symmetric Cones" (1994), Ch. III.

**Relevance:** The key rule is V_1 o V_{1/2} subset V_{1/2}. This means V_1 acts on V_{1/2} as a module. For h_3(O) with V_1 = R * E_{11}, this action is: (alpha * E_{11}) o x = (alpha/2) * x for x in V_{1/2}. The action is scalar -- no room for complex structure transmission through this channel alone.

**v8.0 insight:** The action V_{1/2} o V_{1/2} subset V_1 + V_0 is equally important. When the observer measures an element of V_{1/2} against another element of V_{1/2}, the result lands in V_1 + V_0. The V_1 component of this product is the "inner product" seen by the observer. If this inner product is C-valued (because the observer is C*), then V_{1/2} inherits complex structure from the inner product structure, not from the Peirce multiplication by V_1.

## Open Problems Relevant to This Project

### Open Problem 1: Module Complexification via C*-Actions

**Statement:** Given a C*-algebra A acting on a real Jordan module M via positive maps, under what conditions does A's complex structure induce a complex structure on M?

**Why it matters:** This is exactly Gap C. No general theorem exists.

**Current status:** No published result addresses this question in the specific form needed. The Alfsen-Shultz theory addresses algebra complexification, not module complexification. The representation theory of C*-algebras on real modules is well-developed (Kadison-Singer, Effros), but the question of "forced complexification" -- where the C*-structure of the algebra NECESSARILY induces complex structure on the module -- appears to be unstudied as a general question.

**Counterexample direction:** M_2(C)^sa acting on R^3 via the spin-1 representation does NOT force complexification of R^3. The representation is R-linear and R^3 does not acquire a complex structure. This suggests that C*-actions do NOT generically force complexification. Something specific to the h_3(O) / Peirce setting would be needed.

**Positive direction:** If the observer's measurements on V_{1/2} generate a C*-subalgebra of End_R(V_{1/2}), and this subalgebra contains an element J with J^2 = -Id, then V_{1/2} acquires a complex structure. The question is whether octonionic multiplication by u (which IS such a J on O and hence on O^2) is accessible to the observer through Peirce measurements.

**Key references:** No specific reference found. This appears to be a genuinely open problem.

### Open Problem 2: Observable Algebra of the Observer on V_{1/2}

**Statement:** What is the algebra of observables that the C*-observer can construct on V_{1/2} through Peirce multiplication and its compositions?

**Why it matters:** If the observable algebra is rich enough (specifically, if it contains a complex structure operator), then complexification is forced. If it is too impoverished (e.g., only scalar multiples of the identity), then no complex structure can be transmitted.

**Current status:** For h_3(O) with V_1 = R * E_{11}, the single-step Peirce multiplication L_{alpha*E_{11}} = (alpha/2) * Id gives only scalars. But COMPOSITIONS of Peirce multiplications by elements of V_{1/2} can produce richer operators. Specifically, for x, y in V_{1/2}, the operator L_x L_y - L_{x o y} acts on h_3(O) and its restriction to various Peirce spaces may be non-trivial. The question is whether such compositions can produce the complex structure operator J_u = left-multiplication by u.

**Key references:** McCrimmon (2004), Ch. 5 (Peirce decomposition); Upmeier (1987), Ch. 2 (Jordan operator algebras).

### Open Problem 3: Farnsworth Bimodule Framework for Gap C

**Statement:** Does Farnsworth's (2025) split Jordan bimodule construction provide the right framework for proving that the observer's C*-nature forces complexification of V_{1/2}?

**Why it matters:** Farnsworth's construction uses bimodules to couple distinct "points" (observers) in exceptional Jordan geometries. V_{1/2} IS the bimodule coupling V_1 to V_0. If the bimodule structure inherits complex structure from the observer point's C*-nature, this would close Gap C.

**Current status:** Very recent (March 2025). The paper has not yet been thoroughly analyzed for its implications on Gap C. The paper addresses n-point geometries (multiple observers), not the single-observer Peirce decomposition. But the mathematical framework may be applicable.

**Key reference:** Farnsworth, arXiv:2503.10744 (2025).
