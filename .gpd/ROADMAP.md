### Phase 23: Entropy Increase under Sequential Products

**Goal:** The entropy behavior of reduced states under iterated Luders sequential products on the SWAP lattice is rigorously characterized -- either monotonic increase is proved, or precise conditions for increase/decrease are identified
**Depends on:** Nothing (entry point, parallel with Phase 24)
**Requirements:** ENTR-01, ENTR-02, CALC-01
**Contract Coverage:**
- Advances: claim-entropy
- Deliverables: Entropy theorem (or counterexample + conditional theorem); explicit 2-qubit entropy calculation
- Anchor coverage: ref-lindblad1975 (quantum H-theorem), ref-paper5 (Luders product), ref-paper6 (SWAP lattice)
- Forbidden proxies: Assuming unital channels (Luders is NOT unital for general a); proving results for unital CPTP and claiming they apply to Luders; DPI without checking unitality
**Success Criteria** (what must be TRUE):
1. The entropy change under one Luders sequential product step is computed explicitly for M_2(C) x M_2(C) (SWAP dynamics), with SymPy verification on 4x4 density matrices
2. Either: monotonic entropy increase is proved for reduced states under iterated Luders products on the SWAP lattice (citing Lindblad 1975 or Attal-Joye 2007), OR precise conditions (initial state class, interaction strength regime) under which entropy increases are identified
3. The non-unital character of the Luders map is addressed explicitly -- no silent assumption of unitality
4. All entropy expressions carry correct units (dimensionless in natural units, or bits with explicit ln 2 factors)

**Backtracking trigger:** If entropy can decrease for generic initial states, document the conditions for decrease and assess whether the selection argument (Phase 26) can still work with conditional entropy increase.

### Phase 25: Self-Modeling Requires Free Energy (KEY PHASE)

**Goal:** It is proved (or a loophole identified) that a self-modeling system in the sense of Paper 5 requires free energy to operate, and free energy requires a non-equilibrium entropy gradient
**Depends on:** Phase 23 (needs entropy result for connecting free energy to gradient)
**Requirements:** LAND-01, LAND-02, LAND-03, VALD-02, VALD-03
**Contract Coverage:**
- Advances: claim-landauer (KEY claim -- this phase determines whether Gap C closes rigorously or remains partial motivation)
- Deliverables: Landauer bound on self-modeling cycle; quantitative bound kT * I(B;M) per update; coherence loophole resolution
- Anchor coverage: ref-landauer1961 (kT ln 2 per bit), ref-bennett1982 (logical reversibility, Maxwell's demon), ref-sagawa-ueda2010 (generalized Jarzynski for feedback control), ref-paper5 (self-modeling axioms, sequential product)
- Forbidden proxies: Handwaving the Landauer bound without quantitative calculation; assuming classical thermodynamics applies without addressing quantum coherence; ignoring that self-modeling involves quantum operations not classical bit erasure
**Success Criteria** (what must be TRUE):
1. The self-modeling cycle (test body B, update model M, test again) is explicitly mapped to an information-processing cycle with quantifiable information flow, and the Landauer bound is applied with a concrete bound: free energy >= kT * I(B;M) per update cycle
2. The coherence loophole is addressed decisively: either (a) quantum coherence cannot substitute for classical free energy in the self-modeling cycle (proved), or (b) a coherence-based self-modeling protocol is constructed that operates in equilibrium (loophole exists)
3. The chain self-modeling -> free energy -> non-equilibrium -> entropy gradient is connected explicitly, with each implication stated as a theorem or clearly labeled assumption
4. The Landauer bound is cross-checked against Sagawa-Ueda generalized Jarzynski framework
5. rho = 0 at thermal equilibrium is verified: I(B;M) = 0 when system is at maximum entropy

**Backtracking trigger:** If the coherence loophole is real (self-modeling can operate in equilibrium), Phase 26 weakens. Document the loophole and assess whether a weaker version of the entropy gradient theorem still holds. The chirality-time result (Phase 24) remains valid regardless.

**Depends on:** Phase 23 (entropy result), Phase 24 (chirality-time link), Phase 25 (Landauer bound)
**Requirements:** GRAD-01, GRAD-02
**Contract Coverage:**
- Advances: claim-gradient, claim-gapc
- Deliverables: Entropy gradient theorem via at least one route (Direct/Boundary/rho selection); Gap C resolution statement; Paper 8 theorem connecting complexification to self-modeling viability
- Anchor coverage: ref-hoffman2015 (FBT, evolutionary interface), ref-paper5 (self-modeling axioms), ref-paper7 (Gap C, 9-link chain update)
- Forbidden proxies: Claiming to derive the Past Hypothesis without stating ALL assumptions; proving complexification algebraically (v6.0 ruled this out); claiming the arrow of time is derived if Phase 25 relied on assumptions about the coherence loophole
**Success Criteria** (what must be TRUE):
1. The entropy gradient theorem is proved via at least one route: (A) Phase 23 entropy increase + Phase 24 time-orientation + Phase 25 Landauer -> gradient, (B) finite block + bounded entropy + non-decreasing -> low-entropy boundary, or (C) rho selection among blocks
2. Gap C is stated precisely as a selection effect: complexification of V_{1/2} is not forced algebraically (v6.0 proved this) but by the experiential measure rho selecting self-modelers, which require time-orientation, which requires chirality, which requires complexification
