A companion paper derived the Standard Model gauge group and its
chiral fermion representation from the exceptional Jordan algebra
$h_3(\mathbb{O})$, conditional on complexifying the real Peirce
half-space $V_{1/2} \cong \mathbb{O}^2$ to
$V_{1/2}^{\,\mathbb{C}} = S_{10}^+$---a step left open (Gap~C)
after four algebraic routes were shown to fail.  This paper proves
two independent results.  First, the Landauer bound on the
self-modeling update cycle, together with an entropy gradient theorem
(three independent routes), shows that every self-modeler requires a
non-equilibrium entropy gradient and hence a time-oriented spacetime.
Second, a three-consequence theorem shows that the single algebraic
choice $u \in S^6$ fixing the gauge group and chirality also requires
spacetime time-orientation for physical Weyl spinors, since
$\Gamma_*$ contains the timelike generator $\Gamma_0$.  Both chains
converge on time-orientation as a shared geometric
prerequisite---connecting particle physics to observer
thermodynamics.  This narrows Gap~C: complexification is necessary
for Standard Model--like observers; whether non-complexified blocks
support non-SM self-modelers remains open.  The Landauer entropy
deficit (${\sim}\,10^{28}$~nats) is 94 orders below Penrose's
gravitational estimate (${\sim}\,10^{122}$~nats); the exhaustion
timescale exceeds the universe age by ${\sim}\,10^{93}$.  The bridge
from observer thermodynamics to particle physics rests not on logical
closure but on structural convergence: two independent arguments
identify the same geometric structure as indispensable.
